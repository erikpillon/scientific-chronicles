### 📅 Scientific Anniversaries
* **May 11, 1997: Deep Blue Makes History** ♟️
IBM’s **Deep Blue** defeated Garry Kasparov in a definitive 6-game rematch. It was the watershed moment when the world realized that machines could play the "long game" better than humans, setting the stage for the AI era we live in today.
* **May 13, 1958: The "Sticky" Patent** 🧶
**Velcro** was officially patented. Inspired by the annoying burrs that stuck to a dog’s fur during a walk, George de Mestral turned a daily nuisance into a revolutionary fastening system.
* **May 16, 1985: A Hole in our Sunscreen** ☀️
British scientists published the discovery of the **Antarctic Ozone Hole**. This finding led to the Montreal Protocol, proving that global scientific cooperation can literally save the atmosphere.

### 🎂 Birthdays & Legacies
* **May 11: [Richard P. Feynman](https://scientific-chronicles.vercel.app/date/05-11.html#richard-p.-feynman.html) (b. 1918)** ⚛️
The bongo-playing, safe-cracking Nobel laureate who made quantum mechanics almost make sense. He famously argued that if you can’t explain something simply, you don’t understand it.
* **May 12: [Florence Nightingale](https://scientific-chronicles.vercel.app/date/05-12.html#florence-nightingale.html) (b. 1820)** 📊
She was the **"Lady with the Pie Chart."** Long before modern data science, she used advanced statistical graphics to prove that poor sanitation was a greater killer than battle wounds.
* **May 16: [Jean-Baptiste-Joseph Fourier](https://scientific-chronicles.vercel.app/date/05-16.html#jean-baptiste-joseph-fourier.html) (d. 1830)** 🌊
The man who gave us the "Fourier Series." Every time you listen to a digital music file or look at a JPEG, you are using his math to break complex signals into simple waves.

### 🔢 Mathematical Quirks: The "Absolute" Prime
Since May 11th is the **131st day** of the year, let's look at the "determined" nature of the number 131:
* **Palindrome Prime:** It reads the same backward and forward.
* **Absolute Prime:** No matter how you rearrange its digits (113, 131, 311), the resulting number is *always* prime.
* **The Consecutive Sum:** It is the sum of three consecutive primes: $41 + 43 + 47 = 131$.
