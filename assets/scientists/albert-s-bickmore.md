Albert S. Bickmore was an American naturalist with a grand vision: to bring the wonders of the natural world to the public in a big, organized way. He wasn't just collecting specimens; he was collecting *support* for what would become one of the world's most iconic institutions. Bickmore was a key figure in the founding of the American Museum of Natural History in New York City. He traveled the globe, exploring and collecting, but his true genius lay in his ability to advocate for and establish a place where scientific research and public education could flourish side-by-side. Essentially, he helped build a giant playground for curious minds, ensuring that discoveries from far-off lands could be shared with everyone, not just academics. A true pioneer of accessible science!
