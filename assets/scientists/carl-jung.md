Early in his career, Carl Jung was Sigmund Freud’s golden boy. But while Freud thought literally everything in the human brain was secretly about repressed desires, Jung wanted to talk about ghosts, myths, and why humans all seem to share the same weird dreams. Eventually, the two had a massive falling out. 

Jung went rogue and completely redefined modern psychology. He gave us concepts that we throw around casually today, like "introvert," "extrovert," and the "collective unconscious"—the idea that humans share a massive, invisible hard drive of ancient memories and symbols. 

When he went through a major midlife crisis, he spent years writing down his wildest hallucinations and dreams in a massive, leather-bound volume called *The Red Book*, keeping it completely secret. The guy was half brilliant scientist, half mystic wizard, and entirely responsible for how we talk about our personalities today. 🧠🔮
