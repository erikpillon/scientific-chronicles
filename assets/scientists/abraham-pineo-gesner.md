Before light bulbs, there was kerosene, and before kerosene, there was Abraham Gesner. This Nova Scotian physician and geologist was a true 19th-century polymath, whose curiosity spanned medicine, geology, and chemistry. While mapping Canada's mineral resources, he became fascinated with petroleum. In 1846, he perfected a distillation process to produce a clean-burning lamp fuel from coal (and later bitumen), which he shrewdly named 'kerosene.' This wasn't just a scientific curiosity; it was a revolution! Kerosene provided a safer, cheaper, and more efficient alternative to increasingly scarce and expensive whale oil, which was driving whales to extinction. Gesner's innovation brought affordable, bright light to homes and businesses worldwide, fundamentally changing daily life and offering a much-needed reprieve to the planet's largest mammals. He truly brightened up the dark corners of the pre-electric age.
