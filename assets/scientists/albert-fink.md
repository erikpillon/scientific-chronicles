Albert Fink wasn't just another engineer; he was the guy who practically built America's railroads, or at least optimized them like a boss. Born in Germany, he brought his genius to the US and left an indelible mark on civil engineering. You might know his name from the 'Fink truss,' a super efficient bridge design that helped span countless rivers and valleys, making train travel safer and faster. But Fink wasn't just about the girders; he was also a wizard with numbers, pioneering cost accounting and rate-setting in the railroad industry. He basically taught railway companies how to be profitable, transforming them from chaotic ventures into organized, money-making machines. So, next time you cross a bridge or ride a train, remember Fink: the brains behind the brawn and the bucks.
