Imagine training your entire life as a hyper-elite physicist, making it through NASA’s insanely tough selection process, and finally becoming the first American woman in space. And what does the press ask you at the conference? "Will you cry when things go wrong on the job?" and "Do you need 100 tampons for a one-week trip?" 

Yeah, the 1980s were a wild time. 

But Sally Ride didn't sweat it. At just 32, she was the youngest American ever to launch into orbit. She flew on the Challenger, operated the shuttle’s robotic arm, and literally retrieved a satellite from space. After retiring from NASA, she spent the rest of her life fiercely advocating for science education, specifically making sure young girls knew they belonged in the lab (or, you know, in orbit). She was an absolute trailblazer who handled intense pressure with effortless cool. 🚀👩‍🚀
