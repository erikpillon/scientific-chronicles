Oliver Heaviside was brilliantly, unapologetically bizarre. He painted his fingernails bright pink, replaced his furniture with giant granite blocks, and suffered from severe hearing loss. He was also entirely self-taught. ⚡️📻

While the academics were fumbling around with clumsy math, Heaviside looked at James Clerk Maxwell's massive, confusing equations for electromagnetism and chopped them down into the four clean formulas we still use today. He invented the coaxial cable (the reason you get a clear TV signal) and figured out how the ionosphere bounces radio waves around the Earth. The scientific establishment hated him because he refused to do math the "proper" way, but his methods were so effective that they eventually had to cave.
