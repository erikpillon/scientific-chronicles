Margherita Hack was an absolute force of nature. Known across Italy as the "Lady of the Stars," she was a brilliant astrophysicist who spent her life analyzing the spectral signatures of distant stars to figure out exactly what they were made of. 

She became the first woman to ever manage the Trieste Astronomical Observatory, transforming it from a quiet academic outpost into a massive, world-renowned research center. But she wasn't just a lab rat. She was fiercely independent, a champion high-jumper in her youth, an outspoken atheist, and an animal rights activist who refused to eat meat her entire life. 

She had this incredible talent for taking wildly complex astrophysics and explaining it so clearly on television that millions of people tuned in just to hear her talk about space. They even named an asteroid after her (8558 Hack). She was brilliant, stubborn, and completely iconic. 🔭✨
