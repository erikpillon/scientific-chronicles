George Herbert, the 5th Earl of Carnarvon, was less a dusty academic and more an intrepid gentleman adventurer with a knack for funding the right people. Bored with British weather and keen on Egypt's ancient allure, he bankrolled Howard Carter's relentless search for Tutankhamun's tomb. In 1922, their patience paid off with one of history's most dazzling archaeological finds. Sadly, the "Curse of Tutankhamun" got a good run in the papers after his untimely death from an infected mosquito bite just months later. Coincidence? Or a pharaoh's last laugh? The jury (and tabloid sales) are still out.
