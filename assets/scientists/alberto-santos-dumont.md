Forget what you thought you knew about early flight; Alberto Santos-Dumont was the real rockstar of the skies! This dapper Brazilian engineer wasn't just building flying machines; he was performing aerial ballets over Paris at the turn of the 20th century. While the Wright brothers were quietly perfecting their craft, Santos-Dumont was publicly soaring around the Eiffel Tower in his dirigibles and early airplanes like the 14-Bis, a feat that arguably marked the first public flight of a heavier-than-air machine in Europe. He even invented the wrist-watch to check his chronometer during flight! A true showman and brilliant innovator, he made aviation glamorous and inspired a generation to look skyward.
