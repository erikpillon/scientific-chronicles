Imagine having so much money you could just... fund an entire archaeological dig. That was George Herbert, the 5th Earl of Carnarvon. Not content with simply admiring ancient artifacts, he teamed up with Howard Carter to do some serious tomb-raiding (we mean, *archaeology*). Their big score? The totally intact tomb of Tutankhamun in 1922. Sadly, the Earl didn't get to enjoy his fame for long, dying just a few months after the grand reveal, sparking all those 'Pharaoh's Curse' rumors. Talk about leaving a dramatic legacy!
