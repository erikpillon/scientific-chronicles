Albert Ghiorso was basically the element hunter supreme, a physicist who spent his career at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory adding new, super-heavy elements to the periodic table. If you've ever wondered where elements like Berkelium, Californium, Einsteinium, or Seaborgium came from, chances are Ghiorso had a hand (or a whole team) in their creation and discovery. He co-discovered an astounding *twelve* transuranic elements, a record no one else has touched! His work involved smashing atoms together in accelerators, a high-stakes game of nuclear peek-a-boo that revealed previously unseen components of matter. He pushed the boundaries of what we thought was possible, proving that with enough ingenuity and a really powerful atom smasher, you can literally create new parts of the universe.
