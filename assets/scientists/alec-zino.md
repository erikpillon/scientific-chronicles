While not a household name, Alec Zino was one of those quiet geniuses working behind the scenes who profoundly changed history! This British electrical engineer was a key figure in the pre- and early WWII development of radar. Working with Robert Watson-Watt, Zino was instrumental in the team that built the Chain Home system, the world's first operational air defense radar network. His expertise in designing and implementing the complex electronic components was vital in turning the theoretical concept of radar into a practical, life-saving technology. Zino's contributions helped give Britain a critical edge in the Battle of Britain, proving that sometimes the most impactful scientists are the ones making sure everything *works*.
