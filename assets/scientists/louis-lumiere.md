Imagine inventing the film industry and then confidently declaring it was just a passing fad. 

In the 1890s, Thomas Edison had invented a way to watch moving pictures, but you had to bend over and look into a tiny, single-person peephole machine. Louis Lumière and his brother Auguste thought that was ridiculous. They wanted a shared experience. So, Louis engineered the *Cinématographe*—a brilliant 3-in-1 device that could record, develop, and project moving images onto a massive screen. 

In 1895, they held the first-ever public movie screening. Legend says when they played a clip of a train pulling into a station, the audience was so terrified by the giant moving vehicle that they literally leaped out of their seats to get out of the way. Despite basically creating modern cinema, Louis refused to sell his cameras, insisting people would get bored of movies in a few months. Spoiler alert: he was very wrong. 🎥🎬
