Roald Amundsen did not care about being a romantic hero; he only cared about winning. 

In 1911, he was locked in a brutal race against British explorer Robert Falcon Scott to be the first human to reach the South Pole. While Scott brought mechanical sleds (which instantly broke in the cold) and ponies (which froze), Amundsen did his homework. He learned survival skills directly from the Inuit, dressed his team in loose animal furs, and relied entirely on highly trained sled dogs. 

Amundsen’s strategy was perfectly, coldly calculated. He made it to the South Pole on December 14, 1911, planted the Norwegian flag, and skied back safely. Scott's entire team tragically perished on the ice. Amundsen later became the first person to fly over the North Pole, too. He was the ultimate apex predator of the ice. 🧊🐕
