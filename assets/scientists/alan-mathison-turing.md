Alan Turing was a genuine polymath and a wartime hero, though tragically recognized too late for his monumental contributions. This brilliant British mathematician laid the theoretical foundations for modern computers with his 'Turing machine' concept, essentially sketching out how any algorithm could be computed. During WWII, his genius was vital in cracking the German Enigma code, a feat that arguably shortened the war and saved countless lives. Despite his monumental contributions, he was persecuted for his homosexuality, leading to a tragic early death. Today, he's revered as the father of AI and computer science, a mind far ahead of his time and an enduring symbol of unrecognized brilliance.
