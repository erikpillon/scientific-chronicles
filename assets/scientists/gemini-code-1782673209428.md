Sir George Everest spent decades running the Great Trigonometrical Survey of India. His job was to map the entire subcontinent with obsessive mathematical precision, fighting through malaria, tigers, and monsoon rains just to measure giant triangles on the ground. 

He was brilliant at it, but he was also famously grumpy and strict about local culture. When his successor finally calculated the height of "Peak XV" and realized it was the tallest mountain on the planet, the Royal Geographical Society decided to name it Mount Everest to honor George. 

George absolutely hated the idea. He actively protested the decision, arguing that his name couldn't be written in Hindi and the local populations wouldn't be able to pronounce it (he actually pronounced his own name *EVE-rest*, not *EVER-est*). They ignored his complaints and named it after him anyway. 🏔️🗺️
