Before plastic became the bane of our oceans and the backbone of modern life, there was Alexander Parkes, a British metallurgist and chemist with a knack for innovation. In 1862, he unveiled "Parkesine," a material derived from cellulose nitrate, which could be molded when heated and retained its shape when cooled. This wasn't just a cool trick; it was the world's first successful man-made plastic! While Parkesine itself wasn't a commercial smash hit (it was a bit flammable, oops), it laid the crucial groundwork for celluloid and all the plastics that followed. Parkes literally invented the material that would change everything, from billiard balls to packaging, proving that sometimes, the future is molded by a single, brilliant idea.
