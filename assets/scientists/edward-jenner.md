Smallpox used to be a terrifying, global nightmare. Then came Edward Jenner, a country doctor who noticed something weird: milkmaids never caught smallpox. They did, however, catch a mild, annoying version from cows called "cowpox." 🐄💉

So in 1796, Jenner did something that would absolutely get his medical license revoked today. He took pus from a cowpox blister on a milkmaid's hand and deliberately scraped it into the arm of an 8-year-old boy. Then, he exposed the kid to deadly smallpox. Luckily, the boy was completely immune. Jenner had just invented the world’s first vaccine (the word even comes from *vacca*, the Latin word for cow). He saved hundreds of millions of lives with a hunch and some farmyard germs.
