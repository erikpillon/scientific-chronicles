If you've ever admired a beautifully crafted watch, you have Abraham-Louis Bréguet to thank. This Swiss-born genius, later naturalized French, was basically the Steve Jobs of 18th-century horology. He invented the tourbillon (a mechanism to counteract gravity's effects on watch accuracy), perfected self-winding movements, and even created the first wrist-worn watch for the Queen of Naples! His creations were so sought after, even Napoleon and Marie Antoinette were clients. Bréguet didn't just make watches; he elevated them to an art form and a pinnacle of precision engineering, making timekeeping both accurate and incredibly stylish.
