Adam Sedgwick was a true rockstar (pun intended!) of early geology. This British clergyman and professor at Cambridge wasn't afraid to get his hands dirty, meticulously mapping and classifying rock layers across Wales and England. He's famous for defining two major geological periods: the Cambrian (ancient life!) and the Devonian (Age of Fishes!). Sedgwick was a geological detective, piecing together Earth's immense timeline long before absolute dating existed. Though initially a critic of Darwin's *Origin of Species*, he later wrestled with the implications of evolution, showcasing the evolving nature of scientific thought itself. He literally wrote chapters of Earth's story!
