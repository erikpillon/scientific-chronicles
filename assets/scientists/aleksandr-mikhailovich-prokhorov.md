If you've ever enjoyed a laser light show, used fiber optics, or had laser surgery, you've benefited from the genius of Aleksandr Mikhailovich Prokhorov! This Soviet physicist was a true titan of quantum electronics. Along with Nikolay Basov, he independently developed the theoretical foundations and practical applications for the maser (Microwave Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation) in the 1950s. This groundbreaking work paved the way for the invention of the laser (Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation), which he also co-developed. For revolutionizing how we create and use electromagnetic waves, Prokhorov shared the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1964, proving that sometimes, the smallest particles can have the biggest impact.
