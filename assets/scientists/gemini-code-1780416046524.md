If you ever feel unproductive, please do not look at Thomas Young's resume. He was the classic "polymath" who casually mastered whatever he looked at. By age 14, he spoke Greek, Latin, Hebrew, Arabic, and several other languages. 

He grew up to become a physician, but he got bored and decided to completely ruin Isaac Newton's legacy. Newton insisted light was made of particles. Young set up a piece of cardboard with two tiny slits and shined a light through it. Instead of two dots on the wall, he got a complex pattern of stripes. Boom: the "double-slit experiment." He proved light moves as a wave, totally upending physics. 

As a fun side hobby? He was one of the first people to successfully translate the Rosetta Stone, figuring out how Egyptian hieroglyphs actually worked. Physics, medicine, and ancient history—all conquered by one guy. 🌊📜
