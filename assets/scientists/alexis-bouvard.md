Alexis Bouvard, born in 1767, was a French astronomer who dedicated his life to the meticulous art of celestial mechanics. He became a student and later a collaborator of the legendary Pierre-Simon Laplace, helping to refine his monumental work. Bouvard's own fame comes largely from his incredibly precise astronomical tables for Jupiter, Saturn, and Uranus. While his tables for Jupiter and Saturn were spot on, those for Uranus kept stubbornly deviating from observations. Instead of dismissing the discrepancies as errors, Bouvard boldly hypothesized in 1821 that an unknown planet was gravitationally tugging on Uranus, causing its erratic orbit. This brilliant inference set the stage for the later discovery of Neptune, proving that sometimes, an anomaly is not a problem but a clue to something much bigger. He laid the mathematical groundwork for one of astronomy's greatest "predictive" triumphs!
