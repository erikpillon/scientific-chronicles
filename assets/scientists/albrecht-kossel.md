Meet Albrecht Kossel, the unsung hero who basically put the 'N' in DNA! This German biochemist was a protein whisperer before proteins were cool, but his real claim to fame was diving deep into the mysterious world of nucleic acids. In the late 19th and early 20th centuries, he meticulously identified and isolated the five main nitrogenous bases—adenine, guanine, cytosine, thymine, and uracil—which are the fundamental building blocks of DNA and RNA. His pioneering work, which earned him a Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1910, provided the chemical framework that would later allow scientists like Watson and Crick to crack the genetic code. Without Kossel, we might still be wondering what makes us, well, *us*!
