Albert Calmette was a prominent French physician and bacteriologist whose groundbreaking work dramatically impacted global public health. A dedicated student of Louis Pasteur, Calmette's most enduring legacy is his collaboration with Camille Guérin on the Bacillus Calmette-Guérin (BCG) vaccine, developed between 1908 and 1921. This vaccine became the first effective protection against tuberculosis, a devastating disease that had plagued humanity for centuries. His dedication to immunology and disease prevention, especially in regions like Indochina where he worked, solidified his reputation as a pioneering figure in the relentless fight against infectious diseases, saving countless lives with his scientific rigor.
