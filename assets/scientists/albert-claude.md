Albert Claude was a Belgian-American cell biologist who basically invented modern cell biology. He was the first to wield the electron microscope to peel back the layers of a cell and show us the awesome organelles tucked inside. Before Claude, cells were kind of a blurry, mysterious soup. After him? We got crystal-clear views of mitochondria, endoplasmic reticulum, and all those tiny powerhouses and factories. This wasn't just about pretty pictures; it totally transformed our understanding of how life's fundamental units actually function. He snagged a Nobel Prize for this groundbreaking work, proving that sometimes, you just need a really good microscope and a whole lot of curiosity to change science forever.
