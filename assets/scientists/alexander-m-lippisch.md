Alexander M. Lippisch was one of those brilliant, slightly eccentric aviation engineers who dared to dream of planes without tails. This German aerodynamicist was a trailblazer, pushing the boundaries of flight with his innovative designs, particularly the delta wing. His work was pivotal in developing rocket-powered aircraft during WWII, like the infamous Messerschmitt Me 163 Komet, which was basically a piloted missile. After the war, he brought his genius to the U.S., where he continued to influence aircraft design, contributing to projects like the Convair F-102 Delta Dagger. Lippisch wasn't just building planes; he was sculpting the future of flight, proving that sometimes, less tail really does mean more speed and agility.
