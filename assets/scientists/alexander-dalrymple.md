Before GPS and Google Maps, navigating the high seas was a wild guess for many. Enter Alexander Dalrymple, a Scottish maverick who decided mapping the oceans for the British Empire was his calling. From his early days with the East India Company charting the Indian Ocean, he developed an obsession with accurate hydrography. He was so good (and perhaps a bit stubborn) that when the Admiralty needed their first official Hydrographer, he was the only logical, if sometimes prickly, choice. He didn't just draw maps; he transformed naval exploration and trade with meticulous charts, making voyages safer and proving that even a cartographer could make waves. He even thought he'd discover a massive southern continent, proving that even the sharpest minds have their "oops" moments.
