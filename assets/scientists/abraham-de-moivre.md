It’s one thing to be good at math. It’s another thing entirely when Isaac Newton tells people to go ask *you* for help. 

Abraham de Moivre was a French Protestant who fled to London to escape religious persecution. He spent his days hanging out in coffee houses, basically doing homework for wealthy gamblers by calculating their odds of winning. Along the way, he casually helped invent probability theory and gave us the bell curve (normal distribution). 

But here is the most metal thing about him: as he got older, he noticed he was sleeping exactly 15 minutes longer every single night. Being a math guy, he calculated the exact date when those 15-minute increments would add up to 24 straight hours of sleep. He predicted he would die on November 27, 1754. 

He went to sleep that day and never woke up. The man literally mathed his own mortality. 📊💀
