Topology is the math of shapes, and Felix Klein was its ultimate grandmaster. He wanted to unify all the different, chaotic branches of geometry into one clean system, which he successfully did in 1872 (the "Erlangen Program"). 

But what he is most famous for is absolute brain-breaking weirdness. You know the Möbius strip? A 2D loop of paper with only one side? Klein figured out how to do that in three dimensions. 

He mathematically described the "Klein bottle"—a closed jar that has no inside and no outside. The neck of the bottle literally passes through its own wall to connect to the base. If you poured water into it, the water would just fall out, because the inside *is* the outside. You can't actually build a real one in our 3D universe without breaking the glass, but the math proves it exists in higher dimensions. Pure, geometrical witchcraft. 🍾🌀
