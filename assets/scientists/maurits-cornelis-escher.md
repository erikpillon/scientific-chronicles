M.C. Escher was not a trained mathematician. In fact, he completely failed his high school math exams. Yet, his artwork is absolutely obsessed over by physicists and topologists to this day. 

Escher had a freakish intuitive understanding of geometry. He drew staircases that infinitely looped back on themselves, water that somehow flowed uphill, and tessellations where birds seamlessly morphed into fish. He took highly complex mathematical concepts—like infinity, hyperbolic geometry, and impossible spatial dimensions—and turned them into mind-bending pencil sketches. 

When serious mathematicians finally saw his work, they were stunned. He was visually proving theorems he didn't even know the equations for. If you’ve ever stared at a drawing and felt your brain actively short-circuit, you probably have Escher to thank. ✏️♾️
