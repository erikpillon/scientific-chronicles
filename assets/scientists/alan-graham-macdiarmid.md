Alan MacDiarmid, a brilliant Kiwi chemist who later became a distinguished American academic, fundamentally changed how we think about plastics. Before his groundbreaking work with Alan Heeger and Hideki Shirakawa, plastics were mostly seen as insulators. But MacDiarmid's team proved that certain polymers could actually conduct electricity, kicking off a revolution in materials science. This discovery paved the way for flexible electronics and countless other innovations, earning him a Nobel Prize and demonstrating that even seemingly ordinary materials can hold extraordinary scientific secrets.
