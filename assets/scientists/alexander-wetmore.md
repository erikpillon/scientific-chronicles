Alexander Wetmore was a man who loved birds so much, he literally became their champion on a global scale. This American ornithologist was a legend, spending decades studying birds from the high Arctic to the deep tropics, often in remote, challenging locations. His fieldwork wasn't just about spotting pretty feathers; he made monumental contributions to avian taxonomy, classification, and paleontology, helping to map the evolutionary history of birds. As the sixth Secretary of the Smithsonian Institution, he steered one of the world's most important scientific and cultural organizations, all while continuing his bird research. Wetmore showed that a lifelong passion for winged creatures could lead to a truly soaring scientific career.
