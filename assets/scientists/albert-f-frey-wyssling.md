Albert Frey-Wyssling was a Swiss botanist with a magnificently hyphenated name who loved getting up close and personal with plants – I mean, *really* close. He was a pioneer in understanding the ultrastructure of plant cells, essentially peering into the microscopic architecture that makes plants tick. Before him, we knew plants had cells, but he showed us the intricate details of cell walls, membranes, and organelles using early electron microscopy. His work laid the groundwork for modern plant physiology, revealing how these tiny structures influence everything from water transport to photosynthesis. So, next time you admire a leaf, give a little nod to Frey-Wyssling for helping us see beyond the green surface.
