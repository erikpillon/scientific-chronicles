Next time you're not battling the plague, you can thank Alexandre Yersin! This Swiss-French physician and bacteriologist, born in 1863, was an intrepid explorer of both the world and the microscopic realm. His most famous achievement came in 1894 during a devastating plague epidemic in Hong Kong. While working in a tiny hut laboratory, under intense pressure and in fierce competition with Japanese scientists, Yersin successfully identified the bacterium *Yersinia pestis* as the causative agent of the bubonic plague. Not content to just name it, he then dedicated much of his life to fighting the disease, developing a serum and helping to establish institutes for its study. Yersin's adventurous spirit led him to Vietnam, where he also played a key role in developing agriculture and rubber plantations. From battling ancient scourges to fostering new industries, Yersin was a true scientific swashbuckler.
