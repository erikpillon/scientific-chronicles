You like airplanes that don't fall out of the sky? You owe a massive "thank you" to Theodore von Kármán. He was the undisputed grandmaster of aerodynamics. ✈️🚀

Back when flying was basically just strapping yourself to a motorized kite and hoping for the best, von Kármán brought actual math to the party. He figured out the wild, swirling air currents that form behind moving objects (the Kármán vortex street), which is vital info whether you're building a supersonic jet or trying to keep a bridge from collapsing in the wind. He also founded the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), meaning without him, NASA wouldn't be driving rovers around on Mars right now.
