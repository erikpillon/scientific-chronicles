Yes, his name was John Snow. And contrary to the meme, he actually knew quite a lot. 

In 1854, a horrific cholera outbreak hit London. At the time, doctors believed diseases were caused by "miasma"—basically, bad, smelly air. Snow thought that was garbage. He believed the disease was in the water. 

He went full detective, mapping out every single death in the Soho neighborhood. He realized almost all the victims were drinking from one specific water pump on Broad Street. He took his map to the local authorities and convinced them to simply remove the handle from the pump so nobody could use it. The outbreak almost immediately stopped. He basically invented modern epidemiology with a map and a wrench. Oh, and he was also Queen Victoria's personal anesthesiologist. The guy was an absolute medical rockstar. 💧🗺️
