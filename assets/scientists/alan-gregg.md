Alan Gregg might not be a household name, but he was a giant behind the scenes in global public health and medical research. As a director at the Rockefeller Foundation, he expertly steered vast sums towards critical scientific endeavors and medical education around the world. He was the strategic mind who understood that investing in science wasn't just about finding immediate cures, but about building the infrastructure, training talent, and fostering the intellectual environment necessary to prevent disease and improve global well-being long-term. Think of him as the ultimate scientific orchestrator, funding breakthroughs that continue to impact millions, even if his name isn't on the lab report.
