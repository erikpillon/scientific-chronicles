Meet Alexis Claude Clairaut, the mathematical whiz kid of 18th-century France! Born in 1713, he was a prodigy who read a paper on four geometric curves to the French Academy of Sciences at just 13 years old. By 18, he was admitted to the Academy, making him the youngest person ever to achieve such an honor. Clairaut was a master of celestial mechanics, famous for his rigorous mathematical analysis of Newton's theory of gravitation. He applied this to determine the shape of the Earth (proving it's an oblate spheroid, flattened at the poles, via an expedition to Lapland) and to meticulously calculate the orbit of Halley's Comet, predicting its return with incredible accuracy. He even developed a "Clairaut's theorem" relating gravity and the shape of a rotating planet. Essentially, he helped smooth out Newton's wrinkles and showed us just how precise mathematical physics could be in mapping the cosmos.
