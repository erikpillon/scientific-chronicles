Alan Hale is an American astronomer whose name became synonymous with one of the most spectacular celestial events of the late 20th century. He's best known for co-discovering the magnificent Comet Hale-Bopp in 1995 (along with Thomas Bopp, naturally). This wasn't just any old chunk of ice and dust; Hale-Bopp became one of the brightest and most observed comets in recent memory, captivating sky-gazers worldwide. Hale's journey, from a former NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory employee to a dedicated amateur astronomer making a professional career, proves that sometimes the biggest discoveries are made by simply looking up with passion and persistence.
