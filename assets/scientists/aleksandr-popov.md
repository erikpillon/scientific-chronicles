Before your smartphone wirelessly connects to, well, everything, someone had to figure out how to send signals through thin air! Enter Aleksandr Popov, a Russian physicist who, in the late 19th century, was quietly tinkering his way towards what we now call radio. While Guglielmo Marconi often gets the superstar treatment, Popov was right there on the scene, making his own breakthroughs. In 1895, he famously demonstrated a radio receiver that could detect lightning strikes from a distance, essentially using electromagnetic waves to do something useful. Then, in 1896, he transmitted a message over 250 meters – two simple words, "Heinrich Hertz," paying homage to the scientist who proved the existence of electromagnetic waves. Popov's work was foundational, proving that wireless communication wasn't just a fantasy but a tangible reality, laying down the groundwork for everything from radio broadcasts to Wi-Fi. He was a silent trailblazer, sending signals into the future.
