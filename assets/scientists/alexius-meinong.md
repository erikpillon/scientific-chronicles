Get ready for a philosophical deep dive with Alexius Meinong! Born in 1853, this Austrian philosopher and psychologist was a student of Franz Brentano and went on to found the Graz School of experimental psychology. But Meinong's true claim to fame (and complexity) is his "object theory." He argued that there are objects that do not exist (like the round square or the golden mountain) but which can still be thought about and have properties. Yes, you heard that right – objects that don't exist! This idea was a massive influence on early 20th-century philosophy, especially on Bertrand Russell (who famously debated and critiqued Meinong's ideas) and Edmund Husserl, shaping discussions on logic, metaphysics, and the nature of reference. Meinong challenged us to think about what "being" truly means, proving that sometimes, the most profound insights come from pondering the things that aren't there.
