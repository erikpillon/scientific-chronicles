Albert Hofmann was a Swiss chemist who, let's just say, stumbled upon something quite revolutionary (and a bit mind-bending) in his lab. While researching ergot fungi for pharmaceuticals in 1938, he synthesized lysergic acid diethylamide, or LSD. Fast forward to 1943, and during a re-synthesis, he accidentally ingested a tiny amount and became the first person to experience its psychedelic effects. Talk about a 'eureka!' moment turning into a 'whoa, what's happening?!' moment. Hofmann spent the rest of his long life studying LSD and other psychoactive substances, convinced they held potential for understanding the human mind. He even rode his bike home on what's now known as 'Bicycle Day,' forever linking him to the psychedelic movement. He lived to be 102, a pretty good run for someone who discovered something so potent!
