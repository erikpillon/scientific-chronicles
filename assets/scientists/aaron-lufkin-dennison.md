Before Aaron Lufkin Dennison came along, watches were often expensive, hand-built masterpieces, lovingly crafted by skilled artisans. If a part broke, you needed a master to fix it, which was costly and slow. Dennison, however, had a brilliant idea: what if we could mass-produce standardized, interchangeable parts, just like Eli Whitney did with muskets? A trained watchmaker himself, Dennison applied these principles to create the American watchmaking industry, founding the Waltham Watch Company in 1850. He basically took watchmaking from a bespoke craft to an industrial marvel, making accurate timekeeping accessible to the masses. His innovations not only built a powerhouse industry but also laid the groundwork for modern precision manufacturing. So, next time you check your perfectly ticking watch, give a nod to Dennison, the guy who made sure you weren't late, affordably!
