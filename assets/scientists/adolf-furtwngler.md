Adolf Furtwängler was less an archaeologist who dug things up, and more a detective who solved ancient art mysteries. This German classical archaeologist and art historian had an almost supernatural ability to identify, date, and attribute ancient Greek sculptures and ceramics. He didn't just admire beautiful objects; he meticulously analyzed their style, technique, and context, bringing a rigorous scientific approach to the study of classical art. Furtwängler’s critical eye allowed him to differentiate between genuine masterpieces and clever forgeries, making him a formidable authority in his field. His work revolutionized classical archaeology by emphasizing systematic documentation and analysis, influencing generations of scholars. He was basically the CSI of ancient Greece, proving that sometimes the greatest discoveries aren't found in the ground, but in the keen observation of what's already been uncovered.
