Albert C. Barnes was a fascinating character who made his fortune through scientific innovation before dedicating his life to art and education. Originally a chemist, he co-invented the antiseptic Argyrol, a silver-protein compound used to prevent eye infections, particularly in newborns. The runaway success of Argyrol made him incredibly wealthy, which he then famously used to amass one of the world's most significant collections of Impressionist, Post-Impressionist, and early Modern art, creating the Barnes Foundation. So, he wasn't just a patron; he was a scientist turned visionary art educator who profoundly shaped both medicine and the art world.
