Ernest Solvay figured out a wildly efficient way to manufacture soda ash—a vital ingredient for making glass and soap. He patented the "Solvay process," built a global industrial empire, and suddenly had more money than he knew what to do with. 

So, what do you do when you're a science nerd with infinite cash? You throw the ultimate VIP party. 

In 1911, he started funding the Solvay Conferences in Brussels. He paid all the expenses to gather the absolute greatest minds on Earth in one hotel to argue about physics. Look up the group photo from the 1927 conference. It’s basically the Avengers of science. You’ve got Albert Einstein, Marie Curie, Niels Bohr, Max Planck, and Erwin Schrödinger just casually hanging out. Solvay’s money directly fueled the debates that birthed quantum mechanics. Not a bad legacy for a guy who made his fortune on soap ingredients. 🧼⚛️
