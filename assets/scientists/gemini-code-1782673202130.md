Up until the 1930s, scientists were extremely confused about how the Sun worked. If it was made of coal, it would have burned out millions of years ago. If it was just shrinking and releasing heat, it still wouldn't last long enough to support life on Earth. 

Enter Hans Bethe. He sat down and casually crunched the math on stellar nucleosynthesis. He mapped out the exact chemical reactions—specifically the carbon-nitrogen-oxygen cycle—that allow stars to crush hydrogen atoms together to make helium, releasing massive amounts of nuclear energy in the process. He literally solved the mystery of starlight. 

He later became the head of the Theoretical Division at the Manhattan Project, calculating the explosive yield of the first atomic bomb. But unlike many of his peers, he spent the rest of his life actively campaigning against nuclear weapon testing. A brilliant mind with an actual conscience. ☀️⚛️
