Abram Lyle might not have worn a lab coat, but his scientific approach to industrial processes changed breakfast forever! This Scottish entrepreneur and sugar refiner was a master of efficiency. Instead of wasting the treacly byproduct of sugar refining (molasses), he figured out how to turn it into a delicious, golden syrup. In 1883, Lyle's Golden Syrup was born, complete with its iconic dead lion and bees motto. He co-founded Tate & Lyle, a name synonymous with sweetness. Lyle proved that innovation isn't just for academics; sometimes, it's about making something wonderful out of something others deem waste!
