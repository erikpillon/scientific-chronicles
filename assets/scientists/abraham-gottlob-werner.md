Abraham Gottlob Werner was the rockstar of geology in his day, famous for teaching at the Freiberg Mining Academy. He championed "Neptunism," the idea that all rocks—yes, *all* of them—were formed by crystallizing or settling out of a primordial ocean. While he was spectacularly wrong about volcanic rocks (his rival James Hutton was right with Plutonism!), Werner was a meticulous observer and brilliant systematizer of minerals. His classifications and teaching methods laid crucial foundations for modern geology, even if his grand theory eventually sank like a stone. He proved that even incorrect theories can inspire generations of scientific inquiry!
