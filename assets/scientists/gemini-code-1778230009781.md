Nicolas-Louis Vauquelin started out as a poor farm kid who just wanted to learn. He managed to snag a job as a lab assistant in Paris, and boy, did he make it count. 🧪💎

He was an absolute master at pulling things apart to see what they were made of. In 1797, he was messing around with a weird red mineral from Siberia and managed to isolate a brand-new element. He named it Chromium, because its compounds made incredibly bright colors (and yes, it’s what makes chrome shiny today). Just a year later, he pulled another new element, Beryllium, right out of an emerald. Not bad for a peasant turned master chemist.
