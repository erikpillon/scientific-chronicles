Albert Heim was the ultimate mountain man, but not in the lumberjack sense – he was a geologist who practically invented the field of Alpine tectonics. This Swiss scientist spent his life scrambling over mountains, meticulously mapping and studying the forces that create these majestic behemoths. He was famous for his detailed diagrams and theories on how the Alps formed, explaining the complex folding and faulting of rock layers. His monumental work, "Mechanismus der Gebirgsbildung" (Mechanism of Mountain Formation), was a total game-changer, showing everyone how Earth's crust could bend and buckle. So, next time you see a jagged peak, think of Heim, who spent his days figuring out how those colossal wrinkles on Earth's face actually got there.
