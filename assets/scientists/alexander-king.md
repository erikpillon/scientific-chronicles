Alexander King wasn't just a chemist; he was a futurist with a serious case of global concern. This British polymath spent his early career in industrial chemistry, but his mind was on bigger explosions: population growth, resource depletion, and environmental collapse. In the late 1960s, he famously co-founded the Club of Rome, a think tank dedicated to tackling humanity's biggest challenges. He didn't just wave a warning flag; he helped produce "The Limits to Growth," a groundbreaking report that shocked the world into realizing that infinite growth on a finite planet was a recipe for disaster. King was the ultimate "big picture" guy, urging us to think sustainably long before it was cool.
