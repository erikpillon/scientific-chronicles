Albert William Stevens was an American aerial explorer who took photography to literal new heights. As a pioneering balloonist and photographer, he wasn't content with just a bird's-eye view; he wanted a sky-high perspective. He was part of the groundbreaking 'Explorer' series of stratospheric balloon flights in the 1930s, setting altitude records and capturing some truly unprecedented images. Most famously, in 1935, he took the first widely published photograph clearly showing the curvature of the Earth from an altitude of over 72,000 feet. This wasn't just a pretty picture; it was scientific proof of our planet's spherical shape from a human vantage point, long before satellites made such views common. Stevens blended daring adventure with scientific rigor, proving that sometimes, you just need to go really, really high to see things differently.
