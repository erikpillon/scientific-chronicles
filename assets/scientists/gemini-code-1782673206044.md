Henry Ford gets all the credit for mass-producing cars, but André-Gustave Citroën was the guy who brought the concept to Europe and made it cool. 

Before he made cars, he visited Poland and bought the patent for a special type of double-helical gear (shaped like a pair of upside-down V's). If you have ever wondered why the Citroën car logo looks like two chevrons, that is exactly why. 

But Citroën wasn't just an engineer; he was an absolute marketing savage. Between 1925 and 1934, he rented the literal Eiffel Tower, rigged it with 250,000 lightbulbs, and used it to flash his company's name across the Paris skyline. It was registered in the Guinness Book of Records as the world's largest advertising sign. He figured out early on that building a great machine wasn't enough—you had to put your name in lights to make people buy it. 🗼🚘
