George Herbert, the 5th Earl of Carnarvon, was a wealthy British lord with a serious passion for ancient Egypt. He wasn't swinging the pickaxe himself, but he was the deep-pocketed patron who funded Howard Carter's legendary excavations in the Valley of the Kings. Without his generous backing, Tutankhamun's glittering tomb might still be lost to history, waiting for another intrepid adventurer. Famously, he died just months after the tomb's opening, fueling all those spooky tales of the 'curse of the pharaohs.' A true grand-scale benefactor who helped uncover one of archaeology's greatest prizes and left an indelible mark on history.
