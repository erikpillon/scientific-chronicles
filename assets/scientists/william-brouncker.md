Sir William Brouncker, the 2nd Viscount Brouncker, was a man of many talents and titles, but in the scientific world, he's best known as the very *first* President of the Royal Society of London, founded in 1660! Talk about setting the bar high. This English mathematician and natural philosopher was a contemporary of Isaac Newton and Robert Boyle, delving into everything from continued fractions (he derived a famous one for pi!) to the rectification of the parabola. He was a true polymath who helped lay the groundwork for modern scientific inquiry in Britain. So, next time you're appreciating a good scientific journal or a well-run research institution, give a nod to Viscount Brouncker – the original ringleader of the scientific revolution in England!
