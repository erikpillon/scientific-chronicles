Abdul Qadeer Khan was a figure draped in controversy and scientific prowess, hailed in Pakistan as a national hero for leading the country's nuclear weapons program. A metallurgist by trade, Khan acquired critical centrifuge technology while working in Europe in the 1970s, which he then used to jumpstart Pakistan's uranium enrichment efforts. His work culminated in Pakistan becoming a declared nuclear power in 1998, a direct response to India's own nuclear tests. However, Khan's legacy is deeply shadowed by accusations that he ran a vast, illicit network selling nuclear secrets and equipment to countries like Iran, Libya, and North Korea, actions that earned him the moniker of a dangerous proliferator from Western nations. A complex character, he was placed under house arrest in Pakistan but remained a revered, if divisive, icon until his death. He built a bomb and then, allegedly, tried to sell the blueprints – talk about a dramatic career!
