Albert C. Gilbert was a true American polymath: an Olympic gold medalist pole vaulter, a wildly successful toy inventor, and a bit of a showman. While he's best known for inventing the Erector Set, the iconic construction toy that inspired generations of engineers and builders, his background included studying medicine and anatomy, which gave him a deep appreciation for mechanics and the human body. He firmly believed in learning by doing, and his toys reflected that hands-on philosophy. Gilbert's unique fusion of science, ingenuity, and a playful spirit made him a titan in the toy industry and a genuine American innovator who knew how to spark curiosity and creativity.
