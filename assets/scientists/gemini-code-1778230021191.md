In the middle of the 20th century, physicists were smashing atoms together and finding all sorts of weird new particles. It was a mess. Abraham Pais stepped in and brought some much-needed order to the chaos. ⚛️🔬

He figured out the concept of "associated production"—explaining why some of these new subatomic particles survived way longer than the math said they should. Beyond being a top-tier physicist, Pais was also just a really cool guy to hang out with. He was Einstein's neighbor at the Institute for Advanced Study, and they used to go on daily walks together. Later in life, Pais used those experiences to write some of the absolute best, most definitive biographies of Einstein and Niels Bohr.
