If you love algorithms, probability, or literally any modern AI, you need to bow down to Andrey Markov. 

In the early 1900s, physicists mostly thought events in the universe were either completely deterministic (clockwork) or completely independent (like flipping a coin). Markov came up with a middle ground. He invented a mathematical concept where the *next* state of a system depends only on its *current* state, completely ignoring the past. 

It’s called a "Markov Chain." To prove his math worked, he didn't use physics—he spent hours manually counting vowels and consonants in a Russian poem by Pushkin to show how letters predictably follow each other. 

Today? Markov chains are the backbone of predictive text on your phone, Google's PageRank algorithm, complex actuarial modeling, and financial market simulations. The man literally mathed out the future by proving that the past doesn't matter. 📊🤖
