If you've ever taken a statistics class and survived, you probably know the name Chebyshev. This guy was a powerhouse in probability theory and prime numbers. But Pafnuty wasn't just sitting in a dusty room doing abstract algebra—he loved getting his hands dirty. ⚙️📐

He was obsessed with kinematics, the study of how things move. He spent years designing weird, fascinating mechanical linkages. He even built a "Plantigrade Machine," a crazy-looking mechanical device that literally walked using a continuous stepping motion, mimicking the way animals move. Math meets steampunk robotics in 19th-century Russia.
