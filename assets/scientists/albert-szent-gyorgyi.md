Albert Szent-Gyorgyi was a Hungarian biochemist who pretty much single-handedly saved countless sailors (and anyone else suffering from scurvy) by isolating and identifying Vitamin C. Imagine, he figured out what was in paprika that kept people healthy! Back in the 1920s and 30s, he was on a quest to understand cellular respiration, and during his experiments, he stumbled upon hexuronic acid, which later became known as ascorbic acid – aka Vitamin C. His pioneering work not only nailed down the chemical structure of this vital nutrient but also explained its biological function, earning him a Nobel Prize. He was a master of connecting the dots, seeing possibilities where others just saw chemical compounds.
