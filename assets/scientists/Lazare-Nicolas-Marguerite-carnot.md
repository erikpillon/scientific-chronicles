Lazare Carnot is what happens when you put a hardcore geometry nerd in charge of a crumbling military. During the French Revolution, the army was falling apart. They handed the mess to Carnot, who applied strict mathematical logic and engineering principles to troop movements, logistics, and fortifications. ⚔️📐

They literally called him the "Organizer of Victory." But when he wasn't busy saving France, he wrote a wildly influential paper on machines and energy loss. His work directly inspired his son, Sadi Carnot, to go on and basically invent the entire field of thermodynamics. Turns out, mathematical efficiency is hereditary.
