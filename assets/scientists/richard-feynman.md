If your mental image of a physicist is a boring guy in a tweed jacket, you haven't met Richard Feynman. He was a Nobel laureate who helped build the atomic bomb, sure. But he also spent his downtime picking top-secret government safes just to mess with security guards, playing the bongos in Brazil, and figuring out the physics of wobbling plates in a cafeteria. 🥁⚛️

Feynman hated pretentious jargon. He believed that if you couldn't explain something in simple terms, you didn't really understand it. To fix the messy math of quantum physics, he just drew little doodles with squiggly lines—now known as Feynman Diagrams—that completely revolutionized how we see particle interactions. He was brilliant, absolutely hilarious, and utterly allergic to authority.
