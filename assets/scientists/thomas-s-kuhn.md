We like to think of science as a smooth, logical staircase where smart people just keep adding new facts until we understand the universe. Thomas Kuhn completely ruined that comforting illusion. 

In 1962, Kuhn wrote *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*. He argued that science actually works in "paradigms." A bunch of scientists agree on a rulebook (like the Earth being the center of the solar system), and they defend that rulebook to the death. When things don't make sense, they just patch the rules. 

But eventually, the math gets so broken that a massive intellectual crisis happens. Then, some young rebel (like Einstein or Copernicus) kicks the table over, burns the old rulebook, and starts a totally new paradigm. Kuhn proved that scientific progress isn't a peaceful climb—it’s a series of brutal, intellectual cage matches. 🥊🔬
