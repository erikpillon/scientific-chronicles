Abram Stevens Hewitt was a true polymath: an engineer, a titan of industry, and even a mayor! He graduated top of his class from Columbia, then dove into the iron business, eventually revolutionizing American steel production by introducing the open-hearth process. This guy wasn't just building an empire; he was pushing industrial science forward. But his ambition didn't stop at factories; he also served as a U.S. Congressman and later as the reformist Mayor of New York City, fighting for public works and education. Hewitt proved you could be both a scientific innovator and a powerful civic leader, shaping both steel and society.
