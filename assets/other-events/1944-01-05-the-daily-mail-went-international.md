The British newspaper, the Daily Mail, went international – transoceanic – on this day in 1944. The Transatlantic Daily Mail, targeting American readers, lasted an impressive two years. Published weekly, it became the first-ever newspaper to cross the ocean. Since its establishment in 1896, the Daily Mail has had a chequered history, with its owner Viscount Rothermere praising Hitler in 1933 and supporting Britain's homegrown fascists.
