The Harbin Ice and Snow Sculpture Festival begins in the chilly north of China, illuminating a city in a blaze of light and ice. The festival features snow sculptures, ice lanterns, ice castles, skiing, figure skating, snow football, and everything from buildings to animals carved out of illuminated ice. Some ice buildings are as much as 50 metres high, and some snow sculptures are as long as two football pitches. The festival apparently began with local fishermen making ice lanterns for ice-fishing.
