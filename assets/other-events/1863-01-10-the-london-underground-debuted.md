The London Underground, the world's first underground railway, made its debut as the Metropolitan Railway. It ran gas-lit wooden carriages hauled by steam locomotives from Paddington Station to Farringdon Street, stopping at Edgware Road, Baker Street, Portland Road, Gower Street, and King's Cross. 38,000 people rode on the first day, and by 1880, it was carrying 40 million passengers a year.
