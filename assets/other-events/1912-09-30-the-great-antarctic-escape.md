On September 30, 1912, six men, part of Scott's ill-fated South Pole expedition, walked out of Antarctica after surviving an entire winter in an ice cave. They had been left in January 1912 and were unable to be picked up by their ship due to pack ice. They spent almost seven months in a snow cave, emerging to hunt, before abandoning their shelter and walking for five weeks across Antarctica. Upon arrival, they learned of Scott's death.
