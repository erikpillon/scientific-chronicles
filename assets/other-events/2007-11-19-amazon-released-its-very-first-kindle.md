Four and a half hours after that, the device sold out. And a technology that many had been talking about for years sprang fully-fledged into life. Jeff Bezos' baby was, of course, not the first e-reader. Project Gutenberg had been making digital books since 1971; books had been available as CD-Roms since the mid-80s; the first ebook readers had been released as early as 1998; and Sony had its own version out in 2006. It was, however, the Kindle that tipped the casual reader away from the notion that books have pages to be turned towards the idea that it might just be easier to pack all the reading you need for a holiday onto one small device.
