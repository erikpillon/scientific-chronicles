Candy corn, a sweet treat, has its own national day. It was created in the 1800s by George Renninger at the Wunderlee Candy Company. Originally named 'Chicken Feed', it was mass manufactured by the Goelitz Confectionery Company (now Jelly Belly) starting in 1898. By the 1950s, advertising made it synonymous with Halloween.
