Traditionally, March 25th was celebrated as 'Lady Day' (Feast of the Annunciation of the Blessed Virgin Mary) and marked the New Year in medieval times. This practice changed in 1752 with the adoption of the Gregorian calendar, shifting New Year's Day to January 1st.
