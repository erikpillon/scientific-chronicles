John Christie opened the Glyndebourne Opera House on this day in 1934, bringing opera to England. He built a theatre and hired talented opera performers, many escaping early Nazi Germany. The institution puts on 120 performances a year, reaching around 150,000 people, and its education program conducts over 230 events annually.
