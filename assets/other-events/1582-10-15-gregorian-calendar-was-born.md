In 1572, Pope Gregory XIII issued a papal decree shortening the year by 10 minutes, 48 seconds, leading to the birth of the Gregorian calendar. This corrected the inaccuracies of the inherited Julian calendar by adding ten days to the current date and making all years exactly divisible by four a leap year.
