On this day in 1953, President Truman announced that the U.S. had developed the H-bomb, or thermonuclear weapon, as part of his final State of the Union address. This bomb could reach temperatures of 50 million degrees Fahrenheit and inflict exponentially more destruction than the bomb dropped on Hiroshima.
