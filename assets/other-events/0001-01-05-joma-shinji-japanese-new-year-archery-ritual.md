The Joma Shinji Japanese New Year archery ritual, based on the Japanese belief that arrows have the power to banish evil, takes place at the Tsurugaoka Hachimangu Shrine in Kamakura. The ceremony dates back to between 1185 and 1333, when Kamakura was Japan's military capital. Archers dressed as samurai shoot at circular targets 27 metres away, with each hit thought to drive the devil away.
