Norwegian-British author Roald Dahl, known for stories like Charlie and the Chocolate Factory, James and the Giant Peach, Matilda, and The Witches, was born in Llandaff, Cardiff, on this day in 1916. He also piloted fighters during World War II and nursed his first wife through a long recovery from brain injury.
