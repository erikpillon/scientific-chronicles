This national day originates from the days before refrigeration when butchers ate leftover or less popular cuts on Fridays to prevent spoilage. Smoking meats like brisket was also a way to preserve them and reduce waste. Brisket is a tough cut of beef or veal, best slow-cooked or barbecued. Black's Barbecue in Lockhart, Texas, became the first restaurant in the 1950s to have a barbecue menu entirely devoted to brisket.
