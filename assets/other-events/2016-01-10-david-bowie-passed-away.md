Music legend David Bowie lost his 18-month battle with liver cancer, two days after his sixty-ninth birthday. Born David Robert Jones on January 8th 1947, he became one of the most influential musicians of the twentieth century. He began his career in the 1960s, releasing "Space Oddity" in 1969. In 1972, he introduced his alter ego Ziggy Stardust. Bowie was a singer-songwriter and actor, known for his role as Jareth in the 1986 film Labyrinth. He was awarded 10 platinum, 11 gold, and 8 silver album certifications, and released 11 number one albums. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1996 and received the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 2006. He declined a CBE in 2000 and a knighthood in 2003.
