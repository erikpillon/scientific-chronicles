The National Health Service (NHS), a comprehensive health service funded by taxation and available to all UK residents, came into effect. Conceived as part of the post-WWII rebuilding, it was shaped by the 1942 Beveridge Report and established by The National Health Service Act, which received royal assent in November 1946.
