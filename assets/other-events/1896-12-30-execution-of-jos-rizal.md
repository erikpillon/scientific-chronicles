On December 30, 1896, José Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines, was executed by Spanish troops at the age of 35. A polymath, he was an artist, poet, writer, fencer, doctor specializing in ophthalmology, and a campaigner for Filipino nationhood, freedom, and education. Rizal Day is celebrated annually in his honor.
