The ancient pagan festival of May Day is celebrated throughout the northern hemisphere. It is about welcoming spring, chasing away evil spirits, and in Germany, relief that Witches' Night (Walpurgisnacht) is over. Communities raise maypoles and hold festivities. The earliest celebrations can be traced back to the Floralia, the festival of Flora, the Roman goddess of flowers.
