Thomas Edison demonstrated the kinetophone, a device that combined moving images and sounds in a public setting, paving the way for 'talkies'. Early challenges included syncing picture and sound, and the poor quality of the phonograph sound. A fire in 1914 destroyed Edison's masters, leading to the abandonment of the system until sound film was resurrected in the 1930s.
