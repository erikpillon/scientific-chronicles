At 9am on this day, the Laki volcano in Iceland erupted, causing an epic disaster. It ejected 600 sq. km. of lava, but the killer was the gases: fluorine gas fell as hydrofluoric acid, and a toxic cloud of sulphur dioxide and sulphuric acid drifted south. This killed plants, livestock (wiping out 20% of Iceland's population), and brought savage winters globally, killing at least 20,000 Britons. The toxic fog in England made the sun appear pale and blood-red.
