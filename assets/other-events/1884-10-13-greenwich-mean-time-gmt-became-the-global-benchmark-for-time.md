On this day in 1884, after a lengthy conference convened by the United States, Greenwich Mean Time (GMT), or the time in London's Greenwich observatory, became the benchmark for time around the world. This resolved issues with navigation caused by nations using different zero degrees longitude points on maps. Noon in Greenwich subsequently set the time zones for the rest of the world, and the line in Greenwich marked the division between east and west.
