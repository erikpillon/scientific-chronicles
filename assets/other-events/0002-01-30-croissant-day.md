This flaky pastry was created in Vienna or Budapest around 1683-1686. Bakers heard Turks attempting to invade the city by tunnelling, leading to the tunnels being blown up and the city saved. In celebration, bakers created crescent moon-shaped pastries to represent the symbol of the Ottoman Empire.
