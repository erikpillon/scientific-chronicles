A huge explosion shook the town of Senghenydd in Wales at 8 AM. More than 900 men were working underground in the local coal mine. Waves of fire and toxic gases swept through the mines, and rescuers were blocked by roof collapses and burning coal dust. By the time rescue attempts were abandoned three weeks later, 440 men and boys were tragically dead. Exactly 100 years later, the National Mining Memorial opened on the site of the Universal Colliery to commemorate those lost.
