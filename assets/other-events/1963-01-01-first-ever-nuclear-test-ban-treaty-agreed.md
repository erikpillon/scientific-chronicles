At the height of the Cold War in 1963, the United States, the USSR, and Britain agreed to the first-ever nuclear test ban treaty. The Moscow treaty banned all nuclear testing in the air, water, or outer space, though it allowed underground tests. This historic move towards world peace was prompted by growing concerns over radiation fallout and its health impacts, and it was later signed by over a hundred other nations.
