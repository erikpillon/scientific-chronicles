Pope Leo X excommunicated Martin Luther from the Catholic Church. Luther had complained about the sale of indulgences and perceived corruption within the Church. His excommunication inadvertently led to the formation of various Protestant churches. Despite being an outlaw, Luther survived, married, had children, and established his church.
