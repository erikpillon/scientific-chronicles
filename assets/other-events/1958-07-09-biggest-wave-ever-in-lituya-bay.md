The biggest wave ever known, a megatsunami over 500m in height, swept through Lituya Bay, Alaska. This event was caused by an 8.3 magnitude earthquake that dumped 30 million cubic metres of rock and ice into the narrow bay, taking out trees high up the mountainsides.
