The Benedictine monk Dom Pérignon allegedly invented sparkling champagne at his abbey in Hautvillers, France. He pioneered a number of champagne innovations, from corks and thicker glass to the successful use of red grapes. Some historians suggest sparkling wine was actually invented and first bottled by the British.
