On this day in 1953, Cochran set one of her 200 aviation records, piloting an F-86 fighter to become the first woman to break the sound barrier. 11 years later, at the age of almost 60, she would fly faster than twice the speed of sound. During World War II, she came close to bringing women pilots into the U.S. Air Force, decades ahead of her time: after the war, she was made a Lieutenant Colonel in the U.S. Air Force Reserve.
