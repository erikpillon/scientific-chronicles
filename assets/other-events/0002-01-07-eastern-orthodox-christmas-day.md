Eastern Orthodox Christmas Day is celebrated on January 7th by some churches following the Julian calendar, marking the birth of Jesus. Many Orthodox churches, including those in America, Albania, Bulgaria, Finland, Greece, and Romania, switched to the Revised Julian Calendar in the early 20th century, celebrating on December 25th. Ukraine recognized December 25th as Christmas Day in 2017, with the first celebration on that date occurring in 2023.
