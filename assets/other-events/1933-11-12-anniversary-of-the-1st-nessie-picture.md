On November 12, 1933, Hugh Gray reportedly photographed a mysterious creature in Loch Ness. While many believe it was a blurred image of an animal like a dog, otter, or seal, this photograph sparked a booming industry around the Loch Ness Monster. Subsequent searches by cryptozoologists have yielded little evidence, with one notable 'find' on March 31 being an April Fool's joke.
