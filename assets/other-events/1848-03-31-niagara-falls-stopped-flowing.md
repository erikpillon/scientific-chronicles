On March 31, 1848, Niagara Falls ceased to flow for two whole days. An ice dam formed at the edge of Lake Erie, preventing water from reaching the falls, causing panic and allowing people to walk on the dried-up riverbed. The flow resumed with a surge of water after the ice dam broke.
