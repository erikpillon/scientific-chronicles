King's Day (Koningsdag) in the Netherlands has been a national holiday and tradition since 1890. The country turns orange to celebrate, with festivities, 'oranjegekte' (orange madness), canal boat parties, and 'vrijmarkt' (free market) where people sell unwanted possessions.
