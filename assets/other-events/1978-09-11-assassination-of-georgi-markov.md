Georgi Markov died of blood poisoning, four days after being stabbed by an umbrella on London's Waterloo Bridge. Markov, a Bulgarian defector, was a writer and broadcaster who made critical broadcasts about the communist regime. The assailant was later revealed as Francesco Gullino.
