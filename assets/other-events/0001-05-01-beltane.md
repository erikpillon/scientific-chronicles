Beltane is the Celtic fire and fertility festival that marks the beginning of summer, just as Samhain (October 31) marks the beginning of winter. In olden times, it marked the astronomical midpoint between the spring equinox and the summer solstice. Today, most pagans celebrate with activities such as bonfires, gathering morning dew, or collecting hawthorn branches.
