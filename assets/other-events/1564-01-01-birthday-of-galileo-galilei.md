Galileo Galilei, a mathematician, physicist, and astronomer, was born. He is considered 'the father of modern science' for setting up a model of rational scientific thought and research. He believed that maths and physics should govern the interpretation of science, published arguments that the Earth revolved around the Sun, invented instruments like thermometers and telescopes, and discovered four of Jupiter's moons.
