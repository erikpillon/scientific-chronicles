In Mexico, Spain, and some Latin American countries, January 6th is known as 'El Día de los Reyes' (The Day of the Kings) or 'El Dia de Reyes' (Three Kings Day). It is the most important day of the festive period, when gifts are traditionally exchanged. The day is celebrated with Rosca de Reyes (King's Cake) in Mexico and Roscón in Spain, a round sweet cake-like bread shaped to signify a king's crown and baked with a small figurine inside representing baby Jesus.
