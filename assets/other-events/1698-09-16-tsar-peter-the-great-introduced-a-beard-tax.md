Tsar Peter the Great introduced a beard tax, ostensibly to impose a more modern way of living on his population at a time when most Western European men went clean-shaven, but in practice, the primary goal was revenue. Anyone who chose to dodge the barbers and preserve his beard had to prove he'd paid the tax by carrying a token.
