On this day in 1856, Queen Victoria established the Victoria Cross, which is the highest award that a British or Commonwealth serviceperson can receive. It is awarded for 'most conspicuous bravery, or some daring or pre-eminent act of valour or self-sacrifice, or extreme devotion to duty in the presence of the enemy.'
