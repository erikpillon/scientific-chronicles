On the evening of May 16th, Operation Chastise commenced, with a British Royal Air Force squadron setting out for the Ruhr valley to destroy three German dams using 'bouncing bombs'. Two large dams were destroyed, but over a third of the 133 men involved died. Squadron leader Guy Gibson was awarded the Victoria Cross.
