The bystander effect, a phenomenon where the more witnesses are present as something bad happens, the longer each individual takes to do something about it, came into being after the brutal murder of Kitty Genovese in Queens, New York, on this day in 1964. Psychological researchers investigated the incident, leading to the concept.
