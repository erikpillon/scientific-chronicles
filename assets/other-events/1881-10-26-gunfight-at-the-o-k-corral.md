The now-famous Gunfight at the O.K. Corral was a shootout between lawmen (Doc Holliday and brothers Virgil, Morgan and Wyatt Earp) and a group of outlaws called the Cowboys (Billy Claiborne, brothers Ike and Billy Clanton, and brothers Tom and Frank McLaury). It occurred at Tombstone, Arizona, USA. Some 30 shots were fired in about 30 seconds, leaving Billy Clanton and both of the McLaury brothers dead, with Virgil, Morgan and Doc Holliday wounded. Wyatt Earp was unscathed. The gunfight was recorded in a 1931 biography and made famous by the 1946 film, My Darling Clementine.
