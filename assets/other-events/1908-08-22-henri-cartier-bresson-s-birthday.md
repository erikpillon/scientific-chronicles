The father of photojournalism, Henri Cartier-Bresson was born in Chanteloup-en-Brie, France. His belief that "in photography, the smallest thing can be a great subject. The little human detail can become a leitmotiv" shaped photography for decades, helping found the now-commonplace reportage style.
