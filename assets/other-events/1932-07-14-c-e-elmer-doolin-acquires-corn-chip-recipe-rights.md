In 1932, C.E 'Elmer' Doolin, while travelling in San Antonio, USA, purchased a bag of fried corn chips. He enjoyed them so much that he pawned his mother's wedding ring to buy the rights to the recipe. He began selling them from the back of his Model T Ford before mass-producing them under the name of Frito Corn Chips.
