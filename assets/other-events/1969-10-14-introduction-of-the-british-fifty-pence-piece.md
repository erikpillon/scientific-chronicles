The Royal Mint introduced the British fifty pence piece. Intended to directly replace the ten-shilling note in the run-up to decimalisation, its bizarre, seven-sided shape was designed to prevent confusion with other round coins. In 1997, the coin shrank, reflecting its shrinking value.
