The oldest film production company in America, Universal Studios, was established today in 1912 as the Universal Film Manufacturing Company, by German-Jewish immigrant Carl Laemmle. He was motivated by irritation at Thomas Edison's practices. Universal survives today in a much-changed form, including theme parks and film production/distribution arms.
