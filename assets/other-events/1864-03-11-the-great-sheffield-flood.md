The greatest manmade flood in British history occurred when the brand new Dyke Dam collapsed just before midnight, unleashing over two billion litres of water onto the Loxley Valley. The disaster killed more than 200 people and washed away hundreds of buildings. Workers had spotted a crack earlier, allowing some villagers to prepare for the disaster.
