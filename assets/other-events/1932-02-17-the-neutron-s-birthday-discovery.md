British physicist James Chadwick published a letter announcing the discovery of the neutron, a breakthrough that paved the way for nuclear fission and, ultimately, the atomic bomb. The neutron makes it possible to split an atom's nucleus, releasing energy through nuclear fission. During the Second World War, Chadwick worked with physicists in the United States on the Manhattan Project, which led to the development of the first atomic bombs. As we confront the prospect of an increasing number of nuclear-armed nations, we may question whether Chadwick's breakthrough is something to celebrate.
