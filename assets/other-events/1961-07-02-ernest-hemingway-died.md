Nobel Prize-winning author Ernest Hemingway died on this day in 1961. He began his career as a journalist and novelist, moving to Paris in the 1920s where he associated with other 'lost generation' figures. His successful novels led him to Key West, where he engaged in big game fishing and writing. Despite his achievements, his later life was marked by heavy drinking, a plane crash, and a battle with depression, leading him to take his own life just before his 62nd birthday.
