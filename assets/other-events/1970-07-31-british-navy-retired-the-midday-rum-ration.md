On this day in 1970, the British Navy retired one of its longest-standing traditions – the midday rum ration, known as a tot. By the time of its abolition, the tot had been reduced to a mere 70 millilitres, but even that came to be considered too much for men who might spend the afternoon managing weapons and complicated machinery.
