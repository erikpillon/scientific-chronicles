Today celebrates the humble apricot, a fruit cultivated in China and central Asia as far back as 2000 B.C. and later introduced to Greece by Alexander the Great. English and Spanish settlers brought apricots to North America in the seventeenth century. Today, most of the world's apricots are grown in the U.S.
