On this day in 1944, the Soviet Army broke through the German blockade of Leningrad, which had lasted for 872 days since September 1, 1941. The city had suffered from cut land communication, air and artillery bombardment, and hundreds of thousands of deaths, with a harsh winter adding to the suffering.
