The British submarine HMS Tiptoe ran aground on a mudbank in the River Clyde. The submarine had been named by Winston Churchill. This was not its first incident; in 1955, Tiptoe hit a steamship in Tromsø Harbour, Norway, and in 1965, it had an incident with HMS Yarmouth off Portland. When it was retired in 1969, a ballerina danced on its deck in honour of its name.
