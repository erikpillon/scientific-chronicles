June 20 (or 21st/22nd) marks the Summer Solstice, the longest day of the year with the most hours of sunlight. Known as 'solstitium' meaning 'sun stands still', it occurs when the Earth's axis tilts closest to the sun. This day is celebrated as Midsummer's night, with traditions including gatherings at Stonehenge to witness the sunrise and significant celebrations in Scandinavia. In the Southern Hemisphere, the Winter Solstice, the shortest day, is celebrated around this time.
