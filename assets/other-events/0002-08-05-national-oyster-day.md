National Oyster Day celebrates oysters, a food source with a long history dating back to the age of dinosaurs. Once a plentiful and cheap food, they became a delicacy due to overharvesting and pollution. New York was a major producer during the 18th and 19th centuries' Golden Age of Oysters, and the town of Oyster Bay was named for them in 1639.
