This day commemorates the Richter Scale, developed in 1935 by seismologists Charles Francis Richter and Beno Gutenberg. The scale records the magnitude of earthquakes, with a 5.0 magnitude earthquake having a shaking amplitude 10 times greater than a 4.0. Earthquakes over 7.0 are considered 'major', and those over 8.0 can cause widespread destruction.
