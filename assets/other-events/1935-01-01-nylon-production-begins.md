On this day in 1935, the synthetic fibre nylon, invented by chemist Wallace Carothers at the DuPont laboratory in Delaware, entered production. Made from coal, water, and air, it was developed as an alternative to silk and first used in toothbrushes, then stockings, and later for wartime applications.
