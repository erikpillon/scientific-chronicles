On this day in 1989, a Boeing 737 crash-landed on England's M1 motorway, after narrowly missing the village of Kegworth. Amazingly, given the plane split into three pieces and that the motorway is busy, only 46 people were killed - 80 people on the plane survived the impact, and drivers somehow managed to avoid the crash. One of the plane's two engines caught fire on the way out of Heathrow, forcing the crew to divert to East Midlands Airport for an emergency landing. In the confusion, the crew shut down the wrong engine, causing it to crash. One expert calculated the risk of dying on any given flight at one in seven million.
