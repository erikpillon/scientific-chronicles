Journalist, politician, and freedom campaigner John Wilkes became an outlaw in the UK for publishing a pornographic and arguably libellous poem. He fled to Paris, but later returned to London and jail. He eventually recovered impressively, becoming Lord Mayor of London and a popular figure in America, continuing his campaign for religious and social freedom.
