A 7-meter (23-foot) tall vat holding 610,000 litres (135,000 imperial gallons) of beer ruptured at the Horse Shoe Brewery in London, causing a tidal wave of beer that flooded streets, destroyed two houses and a pub, and resulted in at least eight deaths. The disaster was declared an act of God.
