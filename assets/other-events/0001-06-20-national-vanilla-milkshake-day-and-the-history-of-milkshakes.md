On June 20, National Vanilla Milkshake Day is observed. Historically, the first milkshake recipes found in 1885 were alcoholic, containing cream, eggs, and whisky. By the 1900s, they evolved into sweet drinks with fruit syrups or chocolate, and eventually ice cream. The invention of the blender led to the smooth milkshake, which became popular in diners and ice cream parlours. Vanilla, the focus of the day, is native to Mexico and comes from the only fruit-bearing orchid.
