A 9.0 magnitude earthquake, the most powerful since records began, struck north-east Japan. This triggered a double tsunami, reaching up to 50 feet high, which swept 10km or more inland, killing over 20,000 people and inundating the Fukushima nuclear power plant, leading to one of the worst nuclear accidents in history.
