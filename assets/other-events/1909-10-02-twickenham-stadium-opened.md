Twickenham Stadium, in southwest London, opened on this day in 1909 with a match between the Harlequins and Richmond rugby union clubs. Constructed on land previously used to grow cabbage, it has been affectionately known as "the Cabbage Patch" ever since. Its agricultural leanings came back to the fore when it was used during World War I to graze cattle, horses and sheep. Owned and operated by the Rugby Football Union (RFU), it is currently the third-largest rugby union stadium in the world and is the home ground of the English rugby union team. Its inauguration capacity of 20,000 spectators has grown to 82,000.
