Duke William II of Normandy's fleet invaded England, meeting the English army under Anglo-Saxon King Harold II near the present-day town of Battle in East Sussex. It was a decisive Norman victory, sealing William's conquest of England. Estimates suggest 2,000 invaders died along with about twice that number of Englishmen. William is said to have founded a monastery on the battle site, with the high altar of the abbey church at the spot where King Harold died.
