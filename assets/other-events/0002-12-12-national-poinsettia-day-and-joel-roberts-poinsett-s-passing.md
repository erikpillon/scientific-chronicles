December 12th was chosen to mark National Poinsettia Day, commemorating the day of Joel Roberts Poinsett's passing in 1851. Poinsett, the first U.S Ambassador to Mexico and a keen botanist, sent cuttings of the plant home in 1828. The plant, originally known as Cuetlaxochitl by the Aztecs, was used for decoration, dye, and medicine.
