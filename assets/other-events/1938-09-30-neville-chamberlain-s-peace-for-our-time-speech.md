On September 30, 1938, British Prime Minister Neville Chamberlain returned from Munich, declaring an agreement with German Chancellor Herr Hitler as symbolic of a desire to avoid war. This statement, famously known as 'Peace for our time,' was quickly undermined as Hitler marched into Czechoslovakia's Sudetenland the next morning, leading to war between Britain and Germany within 12 months. Chamberlain resigned a year later and died by the end of 1940.
