The Alaska Highway, originally known as the Alaska-Canada Military Highway, was an astonishing feat of wartime engineering. Built in a frantic effort to supply Alaska and protect against a potential Japanese invasion during World War II, construction began in March 1942 and was largely completed by October 1942 – a mind-boggling pace! Thousands of U.S. Army Engineers, including both segregated Black and white units, battled permafrost, dense forests, and swarms of mosquitoes to carve a 1,500-mile road through unforgiving wilderness. It was a race against the elements and the clock, showcasing incredible human ingenuity and perseverance to forge a vital strategic link that still serves as a lifeline today.
