Who doesn't love a good jump-scare or a prop seemingly flying out of the screen? 3D movies have been captivating (and sometimes headache-inducing) audiences for well over a century. While the concept of stereoscopic viewing dates back to the 19th century, the first *movie* using the anaglyph (red/blue glasses) process was screened in 1915. However, 3D really had its first major craze in the 1950s, kicked off by films like "Bwana Devil" in 1952, using polarized glasses. Audiences flocked to experience a new dimension of cinema, even if the technology was often clunky. Despite periodic resurgences (like in the 80s and the Avatar-fueled boom of the 2000s), 3D cinema has always promised an immersive future, pushing the boundaries of visual storytelling and giving us an excuse to wear silly glasses in public!
