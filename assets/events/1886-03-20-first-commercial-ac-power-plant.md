Before AC, electricity was a short-range affair, thanks to Thomas Edison's direct current (DC) system. But that all changed when George Westinghouse, with brilliant engineer William Stanley Jr. at his side, flipped the switch on the first commercial AC power plant in Great Barrington, Massachusetts. This wasn't just any old lightbulb moment; it proved that AC could efficiently transmit power over long distances, paving the way for widespread electrification. It kicked off the legendary 'War of the Currents' with Edison, but ultimately, AC’s practicality won out. Thanks to this pioneering plant, our modern world hums with power, delivered right to our homes and businesses, proving that sometimes, the alternating path is the best one to take.
