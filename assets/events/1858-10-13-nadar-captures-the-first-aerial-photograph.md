Before drones, before satellites, and even before the Wright brothers, there was Nadar, and he literally gave us a bird's-eye view of the world! Gaspard-Félix Tournachon, better known as Nadar, was a flamboyant French photographer, caricaturist, and balloonist. In 1858, in an act of daring innovation, he ascended above Paris in his giant hot-air balloon, 'Le Géant,' armed with a bulky camera and glass plates. From this lofty perch, he managed to capture the first-ever aerial photograph – a blurry but undeniably historic view of the city's rooftops. It wasn't easy; dealing with movement, light, and the temperamental wet collodion process in a swaying basket was a monumental challenge. While the original photo is lost, the achievement itself resonated globally. Nadar's pioneering 'air photo' wasn't just a technical feat; it opened up entirely new perspectives for mapping, surveillance, and simply seeing our world differently. He truly showed us that sometimes, you have to get high to get a good shot!
