If anyone ever tries to tell you the Earth is flat, just remind them that a Greek librarian proved it was round over 2,000 years ago using nothing but a stick and basic geometry. 

Eratosthenes was the director of the legendary Library of Alexandria. He read that in a city further south (Syene), columns cast absolutely zero shadow at noon on the summer solstice because the sun was directly overhead. But on that exact same day in Alexandria, a stick placed in the ground *did* cast a shadow. 

He realized this was only possible if the Earth's surface was curved. By measuring the angle of the shadow in Alexandria (about 7 degrees) and hiring a guy to literally walk the distance between the two cities to get the exact mileage, he crunched the math. He calculated the Earth's circumference with shocking accuracy. No satellites, no lasers. Just pure, unadulterated brainpower. 🌍📏
