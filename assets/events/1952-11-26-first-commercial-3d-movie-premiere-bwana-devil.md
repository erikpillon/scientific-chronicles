While early attempts at 3D cinema date back to the 1910s, it wasn't until 1952 that "Bwana Devil" roared onto screens, kicking off the golden age (or rather, a brief, chaotic frenzy) of 3D movies. Directed by Arch Oboler, this jungle adventure used the then-innovative Natural Vision 3D process, requiring audiences to don those iconic red-and-cyan glasses. It was a gimmick, sure, but a wildly popular one for a short while, offering a novel escape from the growing popularity of television. While the trend eventually faded due to technical kinks and audience fatigue, it proved that people were hungry for immersive experiences, laying the groundwork for later cinematic spectacles and a lot of eye strain.
