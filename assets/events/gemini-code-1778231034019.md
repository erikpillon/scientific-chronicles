If you want to talk about rogue medicine, we need to talk about English physician Edward Jenner. Smallpox was an absolute nightmare in the 18th century. But Jenner noticed something weird: milkmaids who caught a mild illness called "cowpox" from their cows never seemed to catch smallpox. 

So, on May 14, 1796, Jenner did something that would definitely violate every modern medical ethics code. He took pus from a milkmaid's cowpox blister and scratched it into the arm of an 8-year-old boy named James Phipps. After the boy recovered from the mild cowpox, Jenner intentionally exposed him to actual smallpox. 

Guess what? The kid didn't get sick. Jenner had effectively hacked the human immune system, proving that exposure to a safe, related virus could train the body to fight off a deadly one. He called it a "vaccine" (from *vacca*, the Latin word for cow). That wildly risky experiment eventually led to the complete global eradication of smallpox. 🐄💉
