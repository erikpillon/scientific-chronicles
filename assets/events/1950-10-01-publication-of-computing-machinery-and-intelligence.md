In his 1950 paper, Alan Turing bypassed the philosophical debate of what 'thinking' means and proposed the 'Imitation Game'—now known as the Turing Test. 

He argued that if a machine can carry on a conversation so well that a human can't tell it apart from another person, then for all practical purposes, the machine is 'thinking.' He predicted that by the year 2000, we'd have machines that could pass the test. He was a bit early, but we're definitely having those conversations now. 💻
