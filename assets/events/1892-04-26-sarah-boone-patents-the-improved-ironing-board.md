Tired of clothes looking less than crisp? So was Sarah Boone! In 1892, this incredible African-American inventor wasn't just ironing, she was innovating. Her improved ironing board wasn't just a slight tweak; it was specifically designed to press the sleeves and bodices of ladies' garments without those pesky creases on the other side. Prior boards were often crude, essentially just flat planks. Boone's design was narrower, curved, and reversible, making it far more practical and efficient for the fashion of the day. Her patent, US Patent No. 473,653, wasn't just a piece of paper; it was a testament to ingenuity in everyday life, significantly upgrading domestic tools and making her a pioneering figure in practical invention. She didn't just iron clothes; she ironed out the problems of ironing itself, leaving us all with a smoother, wrinkle-free legacy. Talk about a clean finish!
