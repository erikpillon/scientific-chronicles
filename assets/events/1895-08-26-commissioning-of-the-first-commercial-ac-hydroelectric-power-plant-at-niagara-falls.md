The 'A.C. power plant' became a reality in truly spectacular fashion with the commissioning of the Niagara Falls hydroelectric plant in 1895. This wasn't just any power station; it was a grand battleground in the infamous 'War of the Currents' between Thomas Edison's direct current (DC) and Nikola Tesla's alternating current (AC), fiercely championed by George Westinghouse. AC won big! The Niagara plant, using massive AC generators designed by Tesla and built by Westinghouse, efficiently transmitted power over long distances to Buffalo, New York. This monumental achievement proved the superior viability and economic wisdom of AC for large-scale electrification, literally powering the Second Industrial Revolution and lighting up the modern world as we know it.
