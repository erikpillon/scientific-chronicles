In 1936, Alan Turing published a paper that defined the theoretical foundations of modern computing. He introduced the concept of the 'Turing Machine'—a simple hypothetical device that could simulate any algorithm. 

He proved that there are some problems that computers can never solve, but he also showed that a single machine could be programmed to do anything a human could do with a set of instructions. It was the birth of software. Before we had computers, we had Turing's math. 💻
