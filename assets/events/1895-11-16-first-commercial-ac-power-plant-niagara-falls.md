Forget DC, because when the Adams Power Plant at Niagara Falls was commissioned in November 1895, it was a massive 'mic drop' moment for alternating current. This wasn't just any power plant; it was the world's first large-scale hydroelectric power station using AC, designed to harness the immense power of Niagara Falls and send it beaming across miles to Buffalo, New York. It was the ultimate victory for Nikola Tesla's AC system, championed by George Westinghouse, in the famous 'War of the Currents' against Thomas Edison's direct current. Suddenly, electricity could travel much further and more efficiently, lighting up cities and powering industries like never before. It was a game-changer that literally electrified the world, proving that AC wasn't just technically superior but also commercially viable, paving the way for the modern power grids we rely on today.
