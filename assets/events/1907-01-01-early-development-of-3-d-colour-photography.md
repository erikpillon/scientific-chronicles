Long before Instagram filters and holographic displays, pioneers of photography were busy trying to add depth and vibrant hues to our static images. The development of practical 3-D colour photos wasn't a single "aha!" moment, but a delightful fusion of advancements in both stereoscopy (making images appear three-dimensional) and colour photography (like the Lumière brothers' Autochrome plates from 1907). Suddenly, a still life wasn't just flat; it had depth! A portrait wasn't just monochrome; it was blushing! This painstaking process involved specialized cameras and viewing devices, but it brought a whole new level of realism to capturing moments, paving the way for our modern visual obsession with immersion.
