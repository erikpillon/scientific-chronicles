In the epic "War of the Currents," Nikola Tesla and George Westinghouse championed Alternating Current (AC) against Thomas Edison's Direct Current (DC). The AC team scored a massive victory in 1886 with the opening of the first commercial AC power plant in Great Barrington, Massachusetts, thanks to the ingenuity of William Stanley Jr. This wasn't just about flipping a switch; it was about proving that AC could efficiently transmit electricity over long distances at high voltages, then "step down" to safer levels for homes and businesses. This plant demonstrated AC's clear advantage, paving the way for the modern electrical grid and electrifying the world, proving that sometimes, the most efficient current wins the day, despite the competition's shock and awe.
