Imagine trying to get a clear signal with your fancy new antenna, only to be plagued by a persistent, annoying hiss that you just can't get rid of – even after cleaning out the pigeon poop! That's exactly what happened to Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson at Bell Labs in 1964. This irritating background noise, initially thought to be a malfunction, turned out to be the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation – the faint, uniform afterglow of the Big Bang itself. It was the definitive proof that our universe started with a bang, not a whimper, and its uniform temperature (roughly 3 Kelvin, hence "3K radiation") told us the early universe was incredibly smooth. Suddenly, cosmology moved from speculation to a precision science, all thanks to some persistent static.
