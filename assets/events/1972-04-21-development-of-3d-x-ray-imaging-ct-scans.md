X-rays were great, but they only gave you a flat, shadow-like image. Imagine trying to find a needle in a haystack with just a silhouette! That's where 3D X-rays, better known as CT (Computed Tomography) scans, came in to save the day. Pioneered independently by Godfrey Hounsfield and Allan Cormack in the early 1970s (earning them a Nobel Prize!), this technology takes multiple X-ray images from different angles and then uses computers to reconstruct a detailed, three-dimensional cross-section of the body. Suddenly, doctors could see organs, bones, and tumors in incredible detail, revolutionizing diagnostics and surgery. It's like having X-ray vision that isn't just a party trick, but a life-saving superpower!
