Imagine having a completely new heart, powered by batteries, keeping you alive. That astonishing reality unfolded on July 2, 2001, when surgeons at Jewish Hospital in Louisville, Kentucky, implanted the AbioCor total artificial heart into Robert Tools, a 59-year-old man suffering from end-stage heart failure. Unlike previous artificial hearts that required external pumps and cables protruding from the body, AbioCor was a marvel of miniaturization, designed to be fully internal, weighing about two pounds and powered by a wearable battery pack. While not a permanent solution—serving primarily as a bridge to transplant or as a destination therapy for patients with no other options—this groundbreaking operation was a monumental leap in biomedical engineering. It offered a glimmer of hope for patients facing imminent death from heart disease and dramatically pushed the boundaries of what's possible in medical technology.
