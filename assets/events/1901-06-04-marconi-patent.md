By 1900, the world was hooked on the telegraph. But it had a massive flaw: you had to string physical copper wire across the entire planet to send a message. If a storm knocked down a pole, your communication network was completely dead. 

Guglielmo Marconi thought the wires were stupid. Drawing on the work of earlier physicists, he figured out how to shoot invisible electromagnetic waves through the air. Everyone thought he was crazy, claiming the curvature of the Earth would block the signals. 

Marconi ignored them. On June 4, 1901, he secured a major reissued patent for "Wireless Telegraphy." A few months later, he successfully blasted a Morse code letter "S" all the way across the Atlantic Ocean, proving the haters wrong. He entirely disrupted the monopoly of the telegraph companies, birthed the radio industry, and laid the literal groundwork for the Wi-Fi you are using to read this right now. 📻⚡
