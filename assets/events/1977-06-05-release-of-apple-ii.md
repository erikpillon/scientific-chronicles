Before 1977, if you wanted a computer at home, you basically had to be a hardcore electrical engineer. You bought a bag of loose parts, soldered them onto a circuit board yourself, and punched in commands using confusing toggle switches. 

Then Steve Wozniak and Steve Jobs dropped the Apple II. 

This thing was an absolute alien artifact compared to everything else on the market. It looked like a friendly consumer appliance in a sleek plastic case. It had a built-in keyboard, it output high-resolution color graphics to your actual TV, and you could load programs via cassette tapes. It even had expansion slots so you could upgrade it later. Sure, it was wildly expensive, but it totally smoked the competition. The Apple II proved computers weren't just calculators for scientists anymore—they were tools for everybody. 💾🍏
