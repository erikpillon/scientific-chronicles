Early aviation was completely unhinged. Planes were basically just canvas and wood held together by hope, and flying in them was dangerous enough. Jumping out of them intentionally? That was reserved for absolute adrenaline junkies. 

Enter Georgia "Tiny" Broadwick. At just 18 years old, she hitched a ride with aviation pioneer Glenn Martin. They flew up to 1,000 feet over Los Angeles, cruising at a blistering 30 miles per hour. Then, Georgia simply stepped off the wing. 

She free-fell for a terrifying 100 feet before her silk parachute finally snapped open, gliding safely down to a park below. She became the first woman to ever jump from an airplane. She went on to do hundreds of more jumps, eventually inventing the ripcord because static lines kept getting tangled in the aircraft. She was a fearless pioneer who proved gravity was just a suggestion. 🪂✈️
