Traditional X-rays give you a flat, two-dimensional shadow, but '3D X-rays' – what we now commonly call CT scans – completely revolutionized medical imaging. Pioneered by Godfrey Hounsfield in the early 1970s (building on theoretical groundwork by Allan Cormack), CT scans use multiple X-ray images taken from different angles. A clever computer then reconstructs these into detailed cross-sectional slices, which can be combined to create a full 3D view of bones, organs, and soft tissues. This non-invasive superpower allows doctors to spot tumors, diagnose injuries, and plan surgeries with incredible precision, effectively giving them x-ray vision in three dimensions! Hounsfield and Cormack even shared a Nobel Prize for it – well deserved, indeed!
