In 1963, Valentina Tereshkova blasted off in Vostok 6, becoming the first woman to orbit the Earth. She spent three days in space, orbiting 48 times—more than all the American astronauts combined at that point. 

A former factory worker and amateur skydiver, she proved that women had the 'right stuff' just as much as men. It would be another 20 years before the US sent Sally Ride into space, but Tereshkova had already shattered the ultimate glass ceiling. 👩‍🚀
