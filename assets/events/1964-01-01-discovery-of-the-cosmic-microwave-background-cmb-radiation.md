Sometimes, scientific breakthroughs happen when you're just trying to get rid of annoying static! That's exactly what happened to Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson in 1964 while testing a new horn antenna for Bell Labs. They kept picking up a persistent, low-level hiss from every direction in the sky, a signal that just wouldn't go away, even after cleaning out "white dielectric material" (aka pigeon poop) from the antenna. Little did they know, they had stumbled upon the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation – the faint, uniform afterglow of the Big Bang itself, now cooled to a chilly 3 Kelvin. This accidental discovery provided crucial evidence for the Big Bang theory, solidifying our understanding of the universe's fiery origin and earning Penzias and Wilson a well-deserved Nobel Prize in Physics in 1978. Talk about productive static!
