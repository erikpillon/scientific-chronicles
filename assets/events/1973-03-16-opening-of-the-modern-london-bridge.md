After the much-loved 1831 London Bridge was meticulously dismantled and shipped off to grace Lake Havasu City, Arizona (yes, really!), a new, rather less romantic but significantly more practical bridge was needed for the bustling capital. The "New" London Bridge, a somewhat less decorative affair than its exported predecessor, was opened by Queen Elizabeth II in 1973. It’s a workhorse of concrete and steel, designed to handle the 20th century's traffic demands, proving that sometimes utility wins over grandeur. While it lacks the historical charm of its forebears, it dutifully connects the City of London with Southwark, ensuring commuters can still get their caffeine fix on time.
