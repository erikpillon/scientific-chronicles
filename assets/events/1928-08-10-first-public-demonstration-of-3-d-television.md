Forget expensive movie tickets; 3D TV has been a dream for a long, long time! While it might seem like a modern invention, the first public demonstration of 3-D television actually happened way back in 1928, courtesy of Scottish inventor John Logie Baird. Yes, the very same guy who gave us mechanical television. He used a technique involving rapidly alternating left and right eye views, along with a special viewer, to create a stereoscopic effect. While Baird's mechanical system wasn't exactly ready for prime time (or even decent resolution), it was an astonishing peek into a future where images could literally pop off the screen. It set the stage for decades of experimentation and countless hours of 3D glasses-wearing fun (or frustration, depending on the movie!).
