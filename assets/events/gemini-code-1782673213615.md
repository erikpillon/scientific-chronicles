If you were a Roman emperor craving a strawberry, you were going to be pretty disappointed. For most of human history, strawberries were tiny, wildly fragrant little forest berries. They tasted great, but you needed a hundred of them just to make a decent snack. 

Then came global trade. Explorers found slightly larger strawberries in Virginia (cultivated by Native Americans) and massive, but totally flavorless, white strawberries in Chile. 

In 1806, an English gardener named Michael Keens decided to play matchmaker. He successfully crossbred the different global varieties and presented the very first modern strawberry to the London Horticultural Society. It had the massive size of the South American berries and the rich, red flavor of the North American ones. Oh, and fun fact: they are the only fruit in the world that wears its seeds on the outside (averaging about 200 per berry). 🍓🌱
