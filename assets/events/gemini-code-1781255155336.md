Galileo Galilei looked through a telescope, saw moons orbiting Jupiter, and realized Copernicus was right: the Earth revolves around the Sun. He wrote a book about it. The Catholic Church was not amused. 

In 1633, the Roman Inquisition dragged the elderly scientist into a terrifying trial. They basically handed him a piece of paper and said, *Read this, or else.* Galileo was forced to formally "abjure, curse, and detest" his own scientific findings. He had to swear that the Earth was the unmoving center of the universe. To really rub it in, the Church sentenced him to permanent house arrest and made him recite penitential psalms once a week for three years. Legend says that as he walked away from signing the confession, he muttered, *"E pur si muove"* (And yet it moves). You can ground the astronomer, but you can't change the orbital mechanics of the solar system. ☀️🔭
