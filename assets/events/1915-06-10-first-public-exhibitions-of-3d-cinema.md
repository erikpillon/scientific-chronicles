While 3D movies feel like a modern phenomenon, they've actually been literally popping off the screen for over a century! The very first public 3D film screening happened way back in 1915 at the Astor Theater in New York City, using the now-classic anaglyph (red-blue glasses) method. Pioneers like Edwin S. Porter and William E. Waddell were experimenting with depth perception in film, trying to immerse audiences even further into their cinematic worlds. Though early attempts were often clunky and short-lived, the irresistible idea of bringing movies to life with depth never truly vanished. From the golden age of 3D in the 1950s to its resurgence in the 2000s, the dream of making films jump right into your lap has always fascinated filmmakers and audiences alike. It turns out, depth really *does* add to the drama!
