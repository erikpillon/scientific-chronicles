In a high-stakes race against the British explorer Robert Falcon Scott, Roald Amundsen and his team of four Norwegians reached the South Pole first. 

Amundsen’s success was due to his meticulous planning and his use of sled dogs (a technique he learned from the Inuit). He left a tent and a letter for Scott, who arrived 33 days later to find he'd been beaten. Amundsen proved that in the most extreme environments on Earth, preparation is everything. ❄️
