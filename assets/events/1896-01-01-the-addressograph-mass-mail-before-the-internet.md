Imagine the late 19th century: businesses booming, but every letter, invoice, and circular needed to be addressed by hand. That's a lot of ink, a lot of time, and a whole lot of sore wrists! Enter Joseph S. Duncan, who in 1896, patented and introduced the Addressograph. This ingenious device wasn't a computer, but a highly efficient mechanical marvel that could rapidly print addresses onto envelopes, statements, and documents using embossed metal plates. It was the original 'mail merge' system, a godsend for companies dealing with large mailing lists. The Addressograph streamlined office operations, making mass communication feasible and affordable, effectively kickstarting the era of direct mail long before 'spam' became a digital dirty word. It truly transformed how businesses interacted with their customers, proving that sometimes the simplest machines make the biggest impact.
