Before July 1687, the universe was a bit of a chaotic mess—at least in our heads. Then Isaac Newton published *Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica*, and suddenly, everything had to follow the rules. Gravity wasn't just a suggestion; it was the law. 

Newton laid out the three laws of motion and the law of universal gravitation, effectively inventing modern physics while everyone else was still trying to figure out why apples fell down instead of up. It’s arguably the most influential book in the history of science, proving that the same math that governs a falling fruit also keeps the planets in their orbits. Talk about a heavy read. 🍎
