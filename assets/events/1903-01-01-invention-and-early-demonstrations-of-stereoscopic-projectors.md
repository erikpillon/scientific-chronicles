The 3D projector, that magical box that makes images jump out at you, has a history far deeper than you might think! While we often associate it with modern cinema, early pioneers like Frederic Eugene Ives were demonstrating stereoscopic projection as far back as 1903. These early systems often involved two synchronized projectors, one for each eye's view, with colored filters (like the classic red and green anaglyph glasses) or polarization to separate the images. It was a dazzling feat of optical engineering for its time, aiming to pull audiences deeper into the cinematic experience, making them feel truly part of the action. This quest for immersive entertainment is clearly anything but new!
