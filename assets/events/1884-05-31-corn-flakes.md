Dr. John Harvey Kellogg ran a strict sanitarium in Michigan. He was convinced that eating bland, entirely unseasoned food was the key to a pure, sin-free life. I'm talking food so boring it made water look spicy. 

One day in 1884, he and his brother W.K. were boiling a pot of wheat to make an easily digestible bread substitute for the patients. They accidentally got distracted, left the pot sitting out, and the wheat went stale. Unwilling to throw it away, they forced the stale wheat through a set of heavy metal rollers, hoping to get sheets of dough. 

Instead, every single grain flattened into its own crispy little flake. They baked them, poured milk over them, and realized they had totally reinvented breakfast. However, W.K. realized normal people wouldn't eat this stuff unless it tasted good. He secretly added sugar, horrifying his brother, started his own company in 1906, and launched a global cereal empire. 🥣🥛
