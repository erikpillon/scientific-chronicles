Imagine trying to fix a persistent, annoying hiss in your radio antenna, only to realize it's the faint afterglow of the universe's birth! That's precisely what happened to Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson in 1964 while working at Bell Labs. They just couldn't get rid of this uniform 'noise' coming from every direction. Unbeknownst to them, a team at Princeton (led by Robert Dicke) had already theoretically predicted exactly such a signal – the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation, a relic from the Big Bang when the universe cooled enough for light to travel freely. This serendipitous discovery provided crucial, smoking-gun evidence for the Big Bang theory, transforming cosmology forever and earning Penzias and Wilson a Nobel Prize. Not a bad day at the office, huh?
