The discovery of the 21-cm hydrogen line in 1951 by Ewen and Purcell was a genuine game-changer for astronomy. Theoretically predicted earlier by H.C. van de Hulst, this faint radio emission comes from neutral hydrogen atoms flipping their electron spin, and it's practically everywhere in the universe! Because this particular radiation can pass through cosmic dust clouds that block visible light, it provided astronomers with an unprecedented way to map the spiral arms of our Milky Way galaxy and study the distribution of gas in other galaxies, revealing structures previously hidden from view. It's like finding a secret channel that lets you see the universe's invisible infrastructure, opening up a whole new cosmic perspective!
