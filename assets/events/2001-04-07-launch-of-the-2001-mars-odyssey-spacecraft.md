Launched in 2001, the Mars Odyssey spacecraft has been diligently orbiting the Red Planet ever since, making it the longest-serving spacecraft around Mars to date! Talk about endurance. Its primary mission? To search for evidence of past or present water and map the surface composition with its suite of instruments. Odyssey famously discovered vast amounts of subsurface ice, dramatically changing our understanding of Martian water potential and paving the way for future human missions. It also acts as a crucial communication relay for rovers like Spirit, Opportunity, and Curiosity. It's an unsung hero of Mars exploration, quietly beaming back data and supporting other missions, proving that sometimes, the longest-running show is indeed the best show.
