The 'New' London Bridge, officially opened in 1973 by Queen Elizabeth II, isn't just a bridge; it's a transatlantic celebrity! You see, the previous 'old' London Bridge (the one from 1831, not the nursery rhyme one that fell down) was famously sold to American entrepreneur Robert P. McCulloch in 1968. It was then meticulously dismantled, shipped, and rebuilt in Lake Havasu City, Arizona, where it stands today. Its replacement, a rather more understated pre-stressed concrete structure, now quietly carries the load of daily London traffic. It's a fantastic tale of urban development, clever engineering, and a rather unique piece of British history finding a very warm retirement in the USA.
