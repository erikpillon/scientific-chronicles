The idea of making images pop off a screen is as old as projection itself, and the 3D projector is where the magic begins. While many worked on stereoscopic projection, a significant early public demonstration using the anaglyph method (those red and green glasses!) was in 1915, showcasing films by Edwin S. Porter and William E. Waddell. These early machines cleverly projected two slightly offset images simultaneously, creating the illusion of depth when viewed through the special glasses. It wasn't always perfect – headaches were a common side effect – but it was a dazzling peek into the future of immersive entertainment, proving that engineers were always dreaming of adding that extra dimension to our visual world.
