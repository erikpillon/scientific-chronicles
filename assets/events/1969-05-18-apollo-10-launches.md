NASA wasn't about to send humans to land on the Moon without a trial run. Enter Apollo 10, the ultimate dress rehearsal for Apollo 11. Launched on May 18, 1969, this mission was identical to the actual Moon landing in every single way, except for the landing part. 

Astronauts Thomas Stafford and Eugene Cernan flew the Lunar Module (affectionately named *Snoopy*) down to just 47,000 feet above the lunar surface. They literally got close enough to scrape the mountains. The joke was that NASA intentionally short-fueled the lander so they wouldn't be tempted to go rogue, touch down, and steal Neil Armstrong's thunder. 

They scoped out the Sea of Tranquility landing site, tested the radar, and made sure all the math actually worked. Then they fired the engines, docked back with the Command Module (*Charlie Brown*), and came home. Without Apollo 10 testing the waters, the historic Apollo 11 landing a couple of months later simply wouldn't have been possible. 🚀🌕
