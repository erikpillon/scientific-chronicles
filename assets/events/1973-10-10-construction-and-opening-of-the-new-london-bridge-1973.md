So, there's the *old* Old London Bridge (the medieval one), the *old* New London Bridge (from 1831), and then there's the *actual* New London Bridge we're talking about! The 1831 bridge was, believe it or not, *sold* to American entrepreneur Robert P. McCulloch in 1968, dismantled, and rebuilt in Lake Havasu City, Arizona. Yep, you read that right. London then got a sparkling new, more functional concrete bridge, which was officially opened by Queen Elizabeth II in 1973 (Prince Philip represented her). While perhaps not as aesthetically grand as its predecessors, this modern iteration was built to handle the city's ever-growing traffic, making it a crucial piece of urban infrastructure for the 20th century and beyond.
