Ever wondered where Western science practically started? Welcome to the Library of Alexandria. Back in 2004, archaeologists struck absolute gold when they uncovered what is basically the world's oldest university.

We're talking 13 massive lecture halls with enough seating for 5,000 students. Forget your modern college lecture halls with squeaky chairs; these had elevated central podiums so the big-name thinkers could flex their ancient knowledge. This is the exact place where Archimedes invented his water pump, Eratosthenes measured the Earth's diameter, and Euclid basically gave birth to geometry. 

For centuries, people thought the whole complex was lost to history (thanks a lot, Julius Caesar). But finding the actual physical rooms where the greatest minds of the ancient world held class? Mind-blowing. 🏛️📜
