On June 5, 1981, a quiet report from the U.S. Centers for Disease Control (CDC) broke the news of a strange cluster of pneumonia cases in young, previously healthy gay men in Los Angeles. Dr. Michael Gottlieb's report described an unusual and severe immune system breakdown – something entirely new and terrifying. This wasn't just a medical anomaly; it was the first official alarm bell for what would soon be recognized as Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome, or AIDS. This initial publication was the somber kickoff to one of the most devastating epidemics in modern history, galvanizing scientists and activists worldwide to understand, treat, and ultimately, fight back against a silent killer that would claim millions of lives and irrevocably change society and medicine.
