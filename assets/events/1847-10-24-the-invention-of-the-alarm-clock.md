Before smartphones, before even bedside radios, there was the alarm clock – humanity's persistent nudge into the day. While the ancient Greeks, like Plato, had clever water-powered contraptions that would whistle or make noise, the first mechanical alarm clock designed to wake a specific person was invented by Levi Hutchins in 1787, though his only rang at 4 AM! It wasn't until 1847 that Antoine Redier in France patented an adjustable mechanical alarm clock. This little invention, designed to combat oversleeping, revolutionized productivity, industrialized society, and has been the bane (and blessing) of sleepyheads ever since.
