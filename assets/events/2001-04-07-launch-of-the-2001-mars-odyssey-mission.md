The 2001 Mars Odyssey mission wasn't just another trip to Mars; it was a marathon! Launched in 2001, this plucky orbiter has been faithfully circling the Red Planet for over two decades, making it the longest-serving spacecraft at Mars. Its primary goal was to map the planet's surface and search for evidence of past or present water and volcanic activity. And boy, did it deliver! Odyssey famously discovered vast reserves of subsurface ice, completely rewriting our understanding of Martian hydrology and boosting hopes for future human missions. It's still up there, silently doing its thing, proving that sometimes, the old guard gets the job done better than any newbie.
