In the summer of 1956, a small group of ambitious scientists gathered at Dartmouth College for a workshop that would forever change the future. Led by John McCarthy, who literally coined the term 'Artificial Intelligence' for the event, this wasn't just a casual meeting; it was a conceptual birthing. They set out with the audacious goal of making machines think, learn, and understand language – basically, to recreate human intelligence. While they didn't build HAL 9000 right there, the workshop established AI as a distinct field of study, outlining its core problems and potential avenues for research. Think of it as the original 'Avengers assemble' for AI, bringing together the brightest minds to tackle what seemed like science fiction. It sparked decades of innovation, hype, and yes, a few 'AI winters,' but fundamentally set the stage for every smart device, recommendation engine, and chatbot we encounter today.
