By the late 19th century, businesses were drowning in numbers, and existing adding machines were slow, cumbersome affairs. Enter Dorr E. Felt, a self-taught inventor who, in 1887, secured a patent for his groundbreaking 'Comptometer'. This wasn't just another adding machine; it was the first commercially successful key-driven calculating machine, designed for speed. Instead of cranking handles, users simply pressed keys to enter numbers, allowing for rapid 'touch method' operation – no need to even look at the keyboard! Felt's invention was a game-changer for offices, accountants, and anyone who needed to crunch numbers efficiently. It dramatically reduced the time and effort required for complex calculations, ushering in an era of heightened productivity and paving the way for later innovations in office automation. It proved that sometimes, the best way to speed things up is to just make it click.
