Long before Avatar made 3D cool again and flatscreens became ubiquitous, John Logie Baird, the same Scottish genius who brought us the first working television, was already tinkering with 3D TV! In 1928, he gave the world its very first peek at stereoscopic television, using his clever setup of rotating discs and alternating images to create the illusion of depth. While it wasn't exactly 'plug-and-play' for the average living room of the era, Baird's pioneering work laid crucial groundwork for future 3D technologies. It showed that even in the nascent days of television, inventors were dreaming big, pushing beyond flat screens to capture a more immersive visual experience. He truly was ahead of his time, proving that some ideas are just too good to stay two-dimensional.
