Before the accordion became a staple of folk music and sea shanties, it needed someone to officially invent it! That credit often goes to Cyrill Demian, who in 1829 in Vienna, patented an instrument he called the "Accordion." While similar instruments existed, Demian's version was novel for its bass buttons and chords, allowing a single instrument to produce a full harmonic sound with a simple squeeze and pull. This portable, versatile, and surprisingly loud instrument quickly caught on, becoming popular in parlors, dance halls, and eventually around the world. It’s hard to imagine certain genres of music without its distinctive, soulful sound!
