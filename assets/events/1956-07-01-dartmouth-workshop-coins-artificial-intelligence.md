In the summer of 1956, a group of brilliant minds gathered at Dartmouth College for a two-month workshop that would change the course of technology forever. John McCarthy, a computer scientist with a knack for catchy phrases, proposed the name 'Artificial Intelligence' for their ambitious new field: making machines smart. This wasn't just a casual get-together; it was a foundational moment where visionaries like Marvin Minsky, Nathaniel Rochester, and Claude Shannon laid out the ambitious goals for AI, from language processing to problem-solving. It's safe to say they cooked up something truly monumental, even if they didn't quite foresee the robots taking over our inboxes just yet. This workshop sparked the quest to build machines that think, learn, and create – a quest we're still deeply engaged in today.
