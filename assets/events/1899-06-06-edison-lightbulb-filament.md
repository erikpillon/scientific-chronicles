Let’s get one thing straight: Thomas Edison did not invent the first lightbulb. Dozens of inventors had already figured out how to run electricity through a wire until it glowed. The problem was that the wire would instantly catch fire or melt, rendering the bulb completely useless after a few minutes. 

Edison's real genius was just making the lightbulb *not suck*. 

He tested thousands of different materials trying to find a filament that would survive intense heat. On June 6, 1899, he was issued a major patent for a new manufacturing process. He realized that if you mixed highly-refractory materials (like rare earth oxides) with carbonized organic stuff (like sugar or asphalt), you could create a filament that conducted electricity beautifully without immediately burning out. It was this kind of relentless, trial-and-error chemistry that took the lightbulb from an unstable parlor trick to something you could safely leave on while reading a book. 💡🔌
