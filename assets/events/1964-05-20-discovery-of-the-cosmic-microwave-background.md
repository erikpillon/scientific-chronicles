In 1964, Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson were bothered by a persistent hiss in their radio antenna. They tried everything to get rid of it, including scrubbing out 'white dielectric material' (that's scientist for pigeon droppings). 

But the hiss remained. It turned out they weren't hearing interference; they were hearing the 13.8-billion-year-old afterglow of the Big Bang. They accidentally stumbled upon the most important evidence for how our universe began. Sometimes, scientific gold is hidden under a layer of bird mess. 🛰️
