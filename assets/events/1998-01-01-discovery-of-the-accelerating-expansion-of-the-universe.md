Imagine tossing a ball into the air, expecting it to slow down and fall, but instead, it just keeps accelerating upwards. That's essentially what astronomers found in 1998 when studying distant supernovae! Two independent teams, led by Saul Perlmutter, Adam Riess, and Brian Schmidt, observed that very distant galaxies were moving away from us faster than expected, indicating the universe's expansion is *accelerating*. This mind-bending discovery, which earned them the Nobel Prize in Physics in 2011, implies the existence of "dark energy," a mysterious force pushing the cosmos apart. It completely upended our understanding of the universe's ultimate fate!
