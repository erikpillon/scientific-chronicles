On June 18, 1983, NASA finally shattered the ultimate glass ceiling. Space Shuttle Challenger launched from Florida, carrying Sally Ride, the first American woman to ever leave the atmosphere. 

While the media was busy asking her ridiculous questions about makeup in orbit, Ride was busy doing actual rocket science. During the six-day mission, she operated the shuttle's robotic arm to deploy massive communications satellites for Canada and Indonesia. 

But here is the weirdest part of the payload: alongside all the high-tech metallurgy experiments, NASA packed a literal ant colony. Scientists wanted to see how the social behavior of ants completely broke down in zero gravity. So, while Sally Ride was busy making history and working complex orbital mechanics, there was a tiny plastic box nearby full of very confused, floating insects. 🚀🐜
