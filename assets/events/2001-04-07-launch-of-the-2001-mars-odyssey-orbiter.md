After a few bumpy rides (hello, Mars Climate Orbiter!), NASA launched the 2001 Mars Odyssey orbiter with high hopes. And boy, did it deliver! Launched in April 2001, this plucky spacecraft arrived at Mars in October of the same year and quickly got to work. Its primary goal? To find evidence of past or present water on the Red Planet, map its mineralogy, and study its radiation environment. Odyssey became a trailblazer, discovering vast amounts of hydrogen (suggesting water ice just beneath the surface!) and creating the most comprehensive thermal map of Mars. It's also been a vital communication relay for later missions like the Mars rovers, making it one of NASA's longest-serving and most successful Martian explorers. Still active today, it's a true space veteran!
