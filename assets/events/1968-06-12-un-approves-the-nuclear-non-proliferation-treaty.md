By 1968, the Cold War had proven that humans are terrifyingly good at building weapons that can vaporize entire cities. The US and the Soviet Union had thousands of nukes, and other countries were scrambling to build their own. 

The UN finally stepped in and drafted the Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons (NPT). The deal was simple: If you already have nukes, promise to eventually get rid of them. If you don't have them, promise never to build them. In exchange, everyone gets to share peaceful nuclear reactor tech. 

It was a massive step for global survival. Of course, politics is messy. France and China initially abstained, and a few countries voted no. But the US, UK, and USSR signed it, setting a massive precedent. It didn’t magically solve world peace, but it definitely slammed the brakes on a race that was headed straight for the apocalypse. 🌍☢️
