Long before emails and instant messages, getting a letter across the country, let alone the world, was a drawn-out affair. Enter airmail, a game-changer that promised to shrink distances and deliver news faster than ever thought possible. The very first official airmail flight took place on February 18, 1911, when a young French pilot named Henri Pequet took to the skies over Allahabad, India, carrying a mere 6,500 letters and postcards a distance of about 13 kilometers. It wasn't the longest journey, but it was a monumental leap! Imagine the excitement: letters soaring above the landscape, defying rivers and mountains, bringing news and sentiments with unprecedented speed. This initial flight, though a novelty, quickly blossomed into a vital postal service, revolutionizing global communication and paving the way for commercial aviation. Suddenly, sending a letter across an ocean wasn't a weeks-long maritime adventure but a matter of days, thanks to those intrepid early pilots and their flying machines.
