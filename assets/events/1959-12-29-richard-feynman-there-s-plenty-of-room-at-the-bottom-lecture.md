In a talk at Caltech, physicist Richard Feynman suggested that it should be possible to manipulate and control things on an atomic scale. 

He talked about writing the entire Encyclopedia Britannica on the head of a pin and building machines that build even smaller machines. He basically predicted nanotechnology decades before it became a reality. Feynman's vision showed that the laws of physics didn't prevent us from working at the atomic level; they just made it interesting. 🔬
