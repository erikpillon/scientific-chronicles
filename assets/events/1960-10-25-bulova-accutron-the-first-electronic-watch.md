Move over, ticking gears! In 1960, Bulova dropped a bombshell on the watch world with the Accutron. This wasn't just a new watch; it was the world's first *electronic* watch, powered by a tiny tuning fork vibrating at 360 Hz. Designed by Max Hetzel, the Accutron delivered an unprecedented level of accuracy for its time (it was accurate to about a minute a month, which was insane!). It made a distinctive, soft hum rather than a tick and even found its way into NASA's space missions. The Accutron ushered in the electronic era of timekeeping, paving the way for quartz watches and forever changing how we measure minutes.
