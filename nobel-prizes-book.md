# Nobel Prizes

## Why Nobel gets so much attention?
The fascination with Nobel Prize winners can be attributed to several factors rooted in human nature and societal dynamics:

1. Recognition of Excellence:

Humans are wired to recognize and appreciate excellence. Nobel Prize winners are individuals who have made extraordinary contributions to humanity in fields like physics, chemistry, medicine, literature, peace, and economic sciences. Their achievements represent the pinnacle of human knowledge and creativity, which naturally captures people's attention and admiration.

2. Inspiration and Aspiration:

Achievements of Nobel laureates serve as a source of inspiration. Their stories often involve years of hard work, dedication, and relentless pursuit of knowledge. People are naturally drawn to such narratives, finding motivation in the idea that extraordinary accomplishments are possible through perseverance and brilliance.

3. Role Models:

Nobel laureates often become role models, especially for aspiring scientists, writers, and peace activists. Their work and life stories provide a roadmap for others, showing what can be achieved with passion, talent, and a sense of purpose. Human beings tend to look up to those who have excelled in their respective fields, and Nobel Prize winners epitomize this excellence.

4. Cultural Significance:

The Nobel Prize is internationally recognized and respected. It has become a part of global culture and history. When someone is awarded a Nobel Prize, it often signifies that their work has had a significant impact on the world. This recognition resonates with people across cultures, making them interested in understanding the achievements and the individuals behind them.

5. Intellectual Curiosity:

Humans have an innate curiosity about the world and a desire to understand complex concepts and discoveries. Nobel Prize-winning work often involves groundbreaking ideas and advancements that capture the curiosity of those interested in the fields related to the laureate’s work.

6. Validation of Human Potential:

Nobel Prize winners represent the limitless potential of the human mind. Their accomplishments remind people of the vast capabilities of the human intellect, fostering a sense of collective pride and reinforcing the belief in what humanity can achieve.

In summary, the fascination with Nobel Prize winners is deeply ingrained in human nature, encompassing our admiration for excellence, our need for inspiration, the search for role models, the appreciation of cultural significance, intellectual curiosity, and the validation of human potential.

## Interesting, Quirky and fun stories

- **Kasimir Frejans**: He discovered the chemical rules of radioactive decay and even found a brand-new element: protactinium. His work was so good that the Nobel Committee decided to give him the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1924. They made the decision. It was locked in. But then, a Swedish newspaper found out early and leaked the story. The Nobel Committee was so furious about the leak that they threw a massive bureaucratic tantrum. To spite the press, they completely revoked Fajans' prize and decided not to award a Chemistry Nobel to *anyone* that year. He was robbed of the ultimate scientific glory purely because of a newspaper spoiler.
